# WC-08-02 · Maintenance Schedule, Equipment Registry, Checklists, Parts & Supplies (Operations)

v2.0 · July 11, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 11, 2026.**

## Purpose

The **Operations** module's sidebar holds a **MAINTENANCE** group. Lesson WC-08-01 covers its first entry, **Issues**, the reactive work-order list. This lesson walks the other four entries so you know which one answers which upkeep question: **Maintenance Schedule** for planned recurring work, **Equipment Registry** for the machines those jobs run against, **Checklists** for the standard inspections a tech follows, and **Parts & Supplies** for the maintenance stock a job consumes. On July 9 all four pages returned **Not Found** when probed by direct URL. On July 11, a credentialed pass reached every one of them through the in-app sidebar, and this guide now documents what each page actually shows. Opening any entry changes nothing.

**Navigation note (important).** These four pages load only through in-app navigation: click the sidebar entry from inside the **Operations** module. Typing the page URL directly into the browser still returns a 404 (the app routes these pages itself), which is exactly what the July 9 probe hit. The pages are real; the front door is the sidebar.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians who have finished WC-08-01 (Maintenance Issues). You should be signed in and able to reach the **Operations** module from the left rail. This is a list-level tour of four pages: what each shows and when you open it, not a record-level deep dive.

## Navigation path

The **MAINTENANCE** group sits in the left sidebar under the **Operations** module. All five entries are one click apart, so from **Issues** you reach the next with a single click. Shorthand used below: `Operations → MAINTENANCE → Maintenance Schedule`, `Operations → MAINTENANCE → Equipment Registry`, and so on. Each page breadcrumbs **Maintenance › [page name]** (**Maintenance › Maintenance Schedule**, **Maintenance › Equipment Registry**, **Maintenance › Maintenance Checklists**, **Maintenance › Parts & Supplies**). Remember: sidebar clicks only; direct URLs 404.

## Steps

One action per step. Hold each new screen for two to three seconds before moving on. Every one of these pages follows the standard WashCentral list shape from WC-01-04: a four-tile KPI band, three insight cards, then a list card with **+ New Record**, **Export**, **Filters ▾**, and a search box. This whole lesson is **[READ-ONLY]**: nothing is clicked inside a list, exported, or created.

1. From any page, go to the **Operations** module and read the **MAINTENANCE** group in the left sidebar. **Expected:** the group lists five entries in order: **Issues**, **Maintenance Schedule**, **Equipment Registry**, **Checklists**, and **Parts & Supplies**. **Issues** is WC-08-01, the work-order list. The other four are this lesson. Below MAINTENANCE the sidebar continues into the **INCIDENT REPORTS** group (**All Reports**, WC-08-03) and the **SCHEDULER** group (**Appointments** and its siblings, WC-08-04), so you can see how the whole Operations menu is laid out.

2. Click **Maintenance Schedule** in the sidebar. **Expected:** the page loads with the breadcrumb **Maintenance › Maintenance Schedule**. Four KPI tiles read **42 Scheduled Tasks**, **6 Due This Week**, **2 Overdue**, and **18 Completed This Month**. Three insight cards follow: **Tasks Completed** (Last 12 months, **42**, **↑ +5%**), **Tasks by Frequency** (**Weekly 40%**, **Monthly 35%**, **Quarterly 15%**, **Daily 10%**), and **Schedule Compliance** (**90% ON TIME**, **5% DUE SOON**, **5% OVERDUE**). The list card is headed **MAINTENANCE · SCHEDULE / Maintenance Schedule** with the line "42 records · preventive maintenance planning" and a **Search maintenance schedule…** box. Columns: **TASK**, **EQUIPMENT**, **LOCATION**, **FREQUENCY**, **LAST DONE**, **NEXT DUE**, **ASSIGNED TO**. Sample rows include **Lubricate conveyor chain** (Conveyor, MLAS, Weekly, next due 2025-06-01, Tech Team) and **Inspect chemical pumps** (Chemical System, All, Monthly). This is the planned-work companion to **Issues**: recurring preventive jobs on a calendar, with **NEXT DUE** the column a manager reads first.

3. Click **Equipment Registry** in the sidebar. **Expected:** breadcrumb **Maintenance › Equipment Registry**. KPI tiles: **4 Total Assets**, **3 Operational**, **1 In Repair**, **0 Retired**. Insight cards: **Asset Growth** (Last 12 months, **84**, **↑ +4%**), **Assets by Type** (**Conveyor 30%**, **Chemical System 28%**, **POS / Tech 24%**, **Other 18%**), and **Equipment Health** (**93% ONLINE**, **5% REPAIR**, **2% RETIRED**). The list card is headed **MAINTENANCE · EQUIPMENT / Equipment List** ("4 records · assets & maintenance status") with columns **ASSET ID**, **NAME**, **TYPE**, **LOCATION**, **SERIAL #**, **INSTALL DATE**, **STATUS**. The four rows name the machines a work order points back to: **Blower System** (Air Dryer, SSNB, operational), **Chemical Pump Array** (Chemical System, MLAS, in repair), **Main Tunnel Conveyor** (Tunnel, MLAS, operational), and **POS Terminal 1** (POS Terminal, MLAS, operational). When a caller reports the air dryer or a tunnel motor, this page names and locates the asset. Two demo-data quirks to know before you lean on this page: every row shows the same **ASSET ID** value (`a1000000`), and **SERIAL #** reads **—** on all rows (both observed July 11; see Notes).

4. Click **Checklists** in the sidebar. **Expected:** breadcrumb **Maintenance › Maintenance Checklists**. KPI tiles: **24 Total Checklists**, **8 Completed Today**, **94% Avg Completion**, **1 Overdue**. Insight cards: **Completion Rate** (Last 12 months, **94%**, **↑ +3%**), **Checklists by Type** (**Daily 50%**, **Weekly 25%**, **Monthly 17%**, **Safety 8%**), and **Checklist Status** (**94% DONE**, **2% PARTIAL**, **4% OVERDUE**). The list card is headed **MAINTENANCE · CHECKLIST / Checklist List** ("24 records · maintenance checklists & inspection forms") with columns **CHECKLIST**, **TYPE**, **LOCATION**, **FREQUENCY**, **LAST RUN**, **COMPLETION %**. Rows include **Daily Opening Checklist** (Opening, MLAS, Daily, 100%), **Daily Closing Checklist** (Closing, MLAS, Daily, 96%), **Weekly Safety Inspection** (Safety, All, Weekly, 88%), and **Monthly Deep Clean** (Cleaning, SSNB, Monthly, 100%). These are the fixed inspection lists a tech runs on a service or a shift walk-through. Do not confuse this page with **Lube Checklists**, which the plan places under the Hardware settings pages, not here.

5. Click **Parts & Supplies** in the sidebar. **Expected:** breadcrumb **Maintenance › Parts & Supplies**. KPI tiles: **142 Total Parts**, **8 Low Stock**, **12 On Order**, **$18,400 Total Value**. Insight cards: **Parts Usage** (Last 12 months, **$18.4k**, **↑ +7%**), **Parts by Category** (**Conveyor 35%**, **Chemical 28%**, **Electrical 22%**, **Other 15%**), and **Parts Availability** (**88% IN STOCK**, **6% LOW**, **6% OUT**). The list card is headed **MAINTENANCE · PARTS / Parts List** ("142 records · spare parts & stock levels") with columns **PART #**, **DESCRIPTION**, **QTY ON HAND**, **REORDER PT**, **UNIT COST**, **VENDOR**, **LAST ORDERED**. Rows include **PRT-001 Conveyor Drive Belt** (4 on hand, reorder at 2, $84.00, BeltPro Inc) and **PRT-004 POS Power Supply** (2 on hand, reorder at 1, $145.00, TechParts). This is the maintenance stock a repair draws from. It is separate from **Chemicals & Supplies** in the Inventory module (WC-04-02), which tracks wash chemistry and retail stock; this page is the parts side of upkeep. **QTY ON HAND** against **REORDER PT** is the pairing to read on a "do we have the part" call.

6. Recap the four pages and where each question lands. **Expected:** you can find all four entries under **Operations → MAINTENANCE** and name each one's job. A recurring preventive task goes to **Maintenance Schedule** (watch **NEXT DUE**). A question about a specific machine goes to **Equipment Registry** (name, type, location, status). The steps a tech follows on a service go to **Checklists** (watch **COMPLETION %**). The parts a repair consumes go to **Parts & Supplies** (watch **QTY ON HAND** against **REORDER PT**). And you remember the route: sidebar in-app, never a direct URL.

**Knowledge check [READ-ONLY].** Two questions. (1) A site manager wants the list of preventive services due this month across the network; which of the four pages in this lesson holds it, and which column do you read? (Answer: **Maintenance Schedule**, the **NEXT DUE** column.) (2) You type `maint-equipment.html` straight into the browser and get "HTTP Error 404. The requested resource is not found." Is the page gone? (Answer: no. These pages only load through in-app navigation; open the **Operations** module and click **Equipment Registry** in the sidebar.)

## Expected results

You can reach all four maintenance pages from the **Operations → MAINTENANCE** sidebar in one click each, read each page's KPI band, insight cards, and columns, and say what each one is for: **Maintenance Schedule** for planned recurring work, **Equipment Registry** for the assets those jobs run on, **Checklists** for the standard inspection steps, and **Parts & Supplies** for the maintenance stock a job consumes. You also know why a direct URL fails where the sidebar succeeds. Opening any of these entries changes nothing.

## Notes and troubleshooting (observed behavior only)

- **Direct URLs 404; the sidebar works.** On July 9 each of the four pages (`maint-schedule.html`, `maint-equipment.html`, `maint-checklist.html`, `maint-parts.html`) returned **Not Found** when opened by direct URL. On July 11 all four loaded normally when reached through the in-app sidebar. If you or a caller hits the 404, navigate in-app instead of pasting a URL.
- **Header count vs. row count.** Three of the four list cards claim more records than the table shows: **Maintenance Schedule** says "42 records" but the footer reads "Showing 1–4 of 4 records"; **Checklists** says "24 records" over 4 rows; **Parts & Supplies** says "142 records" over 4 rows. Only **Equipment Registry** matches (4 and 4). Observed July 11 and reported as a new discrepancy; read the footer, not the header, when you need the real row count in this tenant.
- **Equipment Registry demo-data quirks.** All four asset rows show the same **ASSET ID** (`a1000000`) and an em-dash **SERIAL #**, and the **Asset Growth** card shows **84** against a **4 Total Assets** KPI. Observed July 11 and reported as a new discrepancy; on a real tenant expect distinct asset IDs and serials.
- **Controls present, none used.** Each page carries **+ New Record**, **Export**, **Filters ▾**, and a search box. The usual rules apply: **Export** stays untouched, and **+ New Record** forms are opened only to read a layout, then left with **Cancel** — neither happens in this lesson.
- Orientation cross-reference: the plan notes that **Issue Status Types** (the statuses you see on the **Issues** list) are configured under **Settings → Operations**, covered in Lesson 12.7. That is a settings page, not one of the four in this lesson.
- **Narration status.** The v1.0 narration script for this lesson was authored against the July 9 Not-Found state and narrates the 404s in blocks 2–6. It contradicts the live pages and must be re-authored and re-rendered before any recording (see the narration file's status header).
