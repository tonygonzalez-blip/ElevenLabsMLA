# WC-07-04 · Gamification and the Challenge Board (Team)

v1.1 · July 11, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 11, 2026.** (Challenge Board verified July 9; the Recognition Feed and Pulse Surveys sibling pages verified July 11 via in-app navigation.)

## Purpose

This lesson covers the **Challenges** page under **Team → GAMIFICATION**, the Challenge Board WashCentral uses to run point-based competitions for your attendants. By the end you can open the board, read its five KPIs, tell the **Active**, **All**, and **Completed** views apart, and read a challenge card end to end: its progress, its points, its scope and deadline, who is taking part, and who is leading. This is a view-only walk. You will see how new challenges get created, but you do not create or edit one here, and the sibling pages in the same sidebar group are named so you know where the rest of the program lives.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians who support the Team module. You should be signed in, comfortable with the global header and left-rail navigation from WC-01-01 and WC-01-02, and already inside the **Team** module. No prior gamification experience is assumed. Setting up point values, prizes, or targets is configuration work and is out of scope for this lesson.

## Navigation path

`Team → GAMIFICATION → Challenges` (`game-tasks.html`). The **GAMIFICATION** group sits at the bottom of the Team left rail, below **EMPLOYEES** and **SALES COACHING**.

## Steps

One action per step. Hold each new screen for two to three seconds before moving on. This is a read-only review; do not click **+ New Challenge**, the **Create New Challenge** card, or any **Edit** button.

1. In the left rail, under **GAMIFICATION**, click **Challenges**. **Expected:** the Challenge Board loads. The breadcrumb reads **Gamification › Challenges**, the page header reads **Challenges**, and **Challenges** is highlighted in the sidebar.
2. Read the KPI band across the top of the page. **Expected:** five figures summarize the whole program: **84 Total Challenges**, **3 Active Now**, **18 Completed**, **2 Starting Soon**, and **4 Overdue**. These count every challenge on the board, not just the ones shown below.
3. Look at the **GAMIFICATION** group in the left rail. **Expected:** six sibling pages are listed in order: **Challenges**, **Leaderboard**, **Targets**, **Prizes**, **Recognition Feed**, and **Pulse Surveys**. This lesson stays on **Challenges**; each of the others is its own list page.
4. Below the KPIs, find the **CHALLENGE BOARD** section titled **Active & Upcoming Challenges**, with three view controls at the top right: **Active**, **All**, and **Completed**. **Expected:** **Active** is selected. These three controls only change which challenges the board shows; they filter the view and change no data. The **+ New Challenge** button sits to their right and is not used in this lesson.
5. In the **Active** view, look at the **RUNNING NOW** group and read the first card, **Wash Count Challenge**. **Expected:** the card shows a progress ring at **72%**, the points chip **500 pts**, the line **Network-wide · Ends Apr 30**, a countdown chip **⏱ 3 days left**, and the progress figure **360 / 500 washes** above a progress bar. That is the standard top half of every active card.
6. Read the bottom of the same **Wash Count Challenge** card. **Expected:** a row of participant avatars followed by **24 participating**, and on the right the line **Leading: Jose M. · 84 washes**. So one card tells you the goal, how far the team has come, who is in, and who is out front.
7. Scan the other two **RUNNING NOW** cards. **Expected:** **Upsell Champion** shows **45%**, **750 pts**, **8 days left**, **$2,250 / $5,000 in upsells**, and **20 participating**; **Perfect Attendance** shows **88%**, **300 pts**, **⏱ 1 day left**, **22 / 25 days attended**, **32 on track**, and **6 employees still eligible**. The anatomy is identical from card to card; only the metric and its unit change.
8. Look at the **STARTING SOON** group below **RUNNING NOW**. **Expected:** two upcoming cards. **🎯 Customer Rating Sprint** shows **600 pts**, **Satisfaction · May 1–31**, and **Starts in 4 days**; **⚡ Speed Service Blitz** shows **400 pts**, **Volume · May 5–11**, and **Starts in 8 days**. Each carries a one-line rule and an **Edit** button. Do not click **Edit**.
9. Look at the dashed tile at the end of the **STARTING SOON** row. **Expected:** a **+ Create New Challenge** card with the subtitle **Set objective, points, duration & scope**. This and the **+ New Challenge** button are the two ways a manager opens the challenge-creation form. Do not click either one; creating and saving a challenge is out of scope. **[TRAIN- required, not performed here]**
10. Click the **All** view control. **Expected:** the board re-filters to show every challenge rather than only the active and upcoming ones. The KPI band above does not change. The specific cards this view lists were not part of the July 9 capture: TBD — parked (new parked item, see package notes).
11. Click the **Completed** view control. **Expected:** the board filters to finished challenges, matching the **18 Completed** figure in the KPI band. As with **All**, the individual completed cards were not captured on July 9: TBD — parked (new parked item, see package notes).
12. Click the **Active** view control to return to the default. **Expected:** the board shows the **RUNNING NOW** and **STARTING SOON** groups again, exactly as in step 4. Switching views changed nothing on the board itself.
13. Recap the page. **Expected:** the Challenge Board is a read-only scoreboard. The KPIs count the whole program, the **Active** view shows live and upcoming cards, each card reads the same way, and the sibling pages (**Leaderboard**, **Targets**, **Prizes**, **Recognition Feed**, and **Pulse Surveys**) carry the rest of the gamification program. Creating or editing a challenge belongs to a manager and to a later configuration lesson.

## Expected results

From the **Team** module you can open **Team → GAMIFICATION → Challenges**, read the five KPIs that summarize the program, and switch between the **Active**, **All**, and **Completed** views knowing they only filter what you see. You can read any active card: its progress ring, points, scope and deadline, participants, and current leader. And you can point a manager or a new attendant to the sibling pages without leaving the sidebar. Nothing you do in this lesson changes a record.

## Notes and troubleshooting (observed behavior only)

- The KPI band and the cards below it describe different things. The KPIs (**84 Total Challenges** and the rest) count the entire board; the **Active** view shows only the **3 Active Now** and **2 Starting Soon** cards. The **18 Completed** and **4 Overdue** challenges are not on the default view.
- The three view controls (**Active**, **All**, **Completed**) are filters, not separate pages. The breadcrumb stays on **Gamification › Challenges** and the KPI band stays put while you switch between them.
- Every active card follows the same layout, so once you can read one you can read all of them. Only the metric changes: washes, dollars of upsell, or days attended.
- **+ New Challenge**, the dashed **+ Create New Challenge** card, and the per-card **Edit** buttons all open editing flows. This is a view-only lesson, so none of them is clicked and nothing is saved.
- The **All** and **Completed** views were not captured in the July 9 corpus, so their card contents are parked rather than described. The view controls themselves, and the counts they map to, are confirmed.
- The **Recognition Feed** and **Pulse Surveys** pages are real and were verified live on July 11, 2026. Both load only through in-app sidebar navigation (their direct URLs 404, which is why the July 9 probe missed them). Page-level facts for orientation:
  - **Recognition Feed** (breadcrumb **Gamification › Recognition Feed**) is the shout-out log. KPIs: **1,284 Total Recognitions**, **47 This Week**, **6.2 Avg Reactions**, and a **Top Giver** tile. The feed table runs **GIVER, RECIPIENT, MESSAGE, TYPE** (Peer or Manager), **REACTIONS, DATE, ACTIONS** (a **View** link per row), with **Export**, **+ Post Announcement**, and **Filters ▾** up top and a multi-page pager beneath. **+ Post Announcement** publishes to staff, so it is never clicked while touring.
  - **Pulse Surveys** (breadcrumb **Gamification › Pulse Surveys**) is the staff-feedback surface. KPIs: **2 Active Surveys**, **73% Avg Response Rate**, **412 Responses This Month**, **7.8/10 Avg Engagement Score**. An **Active Surveys** section lists survey cards (**Weekly Pulse Check** and **Manager Feedback Q2**, both **ACTIVE**, plus a **New Uniform Policy** **DRAFT**) with **View Results** and **Edit** buttons and a **+ New Survey** control, and a **LATEST RESULTS** section charts rating splits and an eNPS score (**+43**) with top comment themes. **+ New Survey** and **Edit** open send-adjacent flows and stay untouched.

**Knowledge check:** On the Challenge Board, which KPI tells you how many competitions are live right now, and on a single card where do you look to see who is currently in the lead? If a manager asked you to add a new competition, which control would open that form, and why would you not use it during a view-only review?
