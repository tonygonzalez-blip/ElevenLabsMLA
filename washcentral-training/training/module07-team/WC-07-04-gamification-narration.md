### Narration craft standard (project-wide)

1. **The 1-second visual buffer.** Never speak the instant a screen changes. Each action cue is followed by `<break time="1.4s" />` so the click and transition land before the explanation begins.
2. **Varying sentence length.** Short fragments set against longer explanatory lines; no two adjacent blocks with the same rhythm.
3. **Show, don't read.** Narrate what a tool means for the technician's day, not the labels already on screen.

## Timed script

[00:00 | Step 1 — Open Challenges]
Open Challenges under Gamification. <break time="1.4s" /> This is the Challenge Board. WashCentral turns your everyday targets into a running competition here, with points on the line and a deadline on every one. It's what keeps a crew pushing when the lobby goes quiet.

[00:19 | Step 2 — KPI band]
Start at the top. <break time="1.4s" /> Five numbers sum up the whole program at a glance: how many challenges exist, how many are live, how many finished, how many are about to start, and how many slipped past their deadline. One row, and you know where the program stands.

[00:40 | Step 3 — Sibling pages]
Glance down the Gamification group. <break time="1.0s" /> Six pages sit together: the board you're on, plus the leaderboard, targets, prizes, a recognition feed, and pulse surveys. Today we stay on Challenges. Just know the rest of the program lives one click away.

[00:58 | Step 4 — Board and views]
Now the board itself, Active and Upcoming Challenges. <break time="1.4s" /> Three controls sit up top: Active, All, and Completed. They only change what you're looking at, never the data underneath. And that green New Challenge button? Leave it alone today. Building challenges is a manager's job.

[01:18 | Step 5 — First card, top]
Take the first live card. <break time="1.4s" /> A ring shows how far along the team is. Beside it, the reward in points, who it's for, when it ends, and how many days are left. Then the hard number: washes done against the goal. That's the whole story of the challenge in one glance.

[01:41 | Step 6 — First card, bottom]
Look lower on the same card. <break time="1.2s" /> A cluster of avatars and a headcount tell you who's taking part. Off to the right, the current leader and their score. So a technician can answer two questions fast: how's the team doing, and who's out front.

[02:01 | Step 7 — Other live cards]
The other two live cards read exactly the same way. <break time="1.0s" /> One tracks upsell dollars, another tracks attendance. Only the metric and its unit change from card to card. Learn one card and you've learned them all.

[02:17 | Step 8 — Starting soon]
Below the live ones, the challenges warming up. <break time="1.4s" /> Same shape, but instead of progress you get a start date and the rule for winning. Each one carries an Edit button. That's a manager's control, so we read it and move on.

_[Director: point to the Edit buttons; do not click. Creating or editing a challenge is out of scope for this lesson.]_

[02:36 | Step 9 — Create card]
At the end of that row, a dashed tile invites you to create a new challenge. <break time="1.2s" /> It's the second door into the same builder. We're reviewing, not building, so we leave it closed.

_[Director: do not click the + Create New Challenge card or the + New Challenge button. Never open the create form or Save.]_

[02:51 | Step 10 — All view]
Now try the All view. <break time="1.4s" /> The board widens to every challenge, while the numbers up top hold steady. Notice what didn't happen: nothing was edited or sent. You're just changing the lens.

[03:06 | Step 11 — Completed view]
Switch to Completed. <break time="1.2s" /> Here are the challenges that already wrapped, and the count lines up with the Completed figure you read at the top. This is where you'd look back to see how a past competition landed.

[03:23 | Step 12 — Back to Active]
Click back to Active. <break time="1.2s" /> The live and upcoming cards return, right where they were. Three views, one board, and switching between them never touches a thing.

[03:35 | Step 13 — Recap]
So that's Gamification. <break time="0.8s" /> The KPIs count the whole program, the Active view shows what's live and what's coming, and every card reads the same once you know the pattern. When you need the wider picture, the leaderboard, targets, prizes, recognition, and surveys are right there in the sidebar. Read the board, point people to it, and leave the building to the managers.
