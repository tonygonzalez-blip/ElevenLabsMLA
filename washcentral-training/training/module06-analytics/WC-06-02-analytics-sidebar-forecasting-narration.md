### Narration craft standard (project-wide)

1. **The 1-second visual buffer.** Never speak the instant a screen changes. Each action cue is followed by `<break time="1.4s" />` so the click and transition land before the explanation begins.
2. **Varying sentence length.** Short fragments set against longer explanatory lines; no two adjacent blocks with the same rhythm.
3. **Show, don't read.** Narrate what each group and page is for, not the labels already on screen. The on-screen cursor sweep carries the full page lists; the voice carries the meaning.

## Timed script

[00:00 | Step 1 — Analytics sidebar]
Open Analytics. <break time="1.4s" /> This is the deepest sidebar in the platform. Twenty-two pages. Do not try to memorize them one at a time. Learn the four buckets they fall into, and any page becomes easy to find. The labels down the left are your map.

[00:22 | Step 2 — ANALYTICS group]
First bucket, Analytics. <break time="1.0s" /> Ten pages, and these are the live dashboards: the whole network at a glance, each location, memberships, downsells, retail. One catch worth flagging now. The item named Marketing up here is a single dashboard, not the Marketing group further down. Same word, two places.

[00:48 | Step 3 — Executive Dashboard landing]
The module drops you on the Executive Dashboard. <break time="1.4s" /> You met this one last lesson: the period toggles up top, the location scope right beside them. We are not stopping. Today is about learning what lives all around it.

[01:06 | Step 4 — Downsells]
Click Downsells. <break time="1.4s" /> When a member goes to cancel, the system offers a downgrade to keep them instead. These six cards are the scoreboard for that fight: how many tried to leave, how many you talked into staying, and the dollars you kept. Right now, you are saving better than four in ten.

[01:32 | Step 5 — Downsells reasons and Command Center]
Scroll down for the why. <break time="1.0s" /> The reasons people cancel, ranked, and the offers that win them back. Remember the Command Center flagging members with downsell signals? This is where that play cashes out. Read the top reason, read the save rate, and you know in ten seconds whether the flow is working.

[02:00 | Step 6 — MARKETING group]
Second bucket, Marketing. <break time="1.0s" /> Five pages, and the feel changes. These are reports, not dashboards. Campaign results, who responded, which offers landed, where new members came from, and who you managed to win back.

[02:20 | Step 7 — Campaign Performance]
Open Campaign Performance. <break time="1.4s" /> A summary band on top, a sortable table underneath. Look at the header. No location picker this time. Instead, Filters and Export. That is the tell for a report: you slice it, sort it, and pull it into a spreadsheet. Every page in this group works the same way.

[02:48 | Step 8 — INTELLIGENCE group]
Third bucket, Intelligence. <break time="1.0s" /> Six pages, and this is the analyst's shelf: revenue trends, industry benchmarks, churn prediction, lifetime value, and your online reputation. These are the heavier questions, the ones you dig into rather than glance at.

[03:06 | Step 9 — BI Overview]
Start at B-I Overview. <break time="1.4s" /> Eight cards, and together they read the health of the whole business: recurring revenue, retention, churn, lifetime value, even your net promoter score. When someone asks how the network is really doing, this is the single screen you open.

[03:32 | Step 10 — BI Overview panels]
Keep scrolling. <break time="1.0s" /> A full year of revenue, your plan mix, and the members quietly drifting toward the exit. At the bottom, the Deep Dives. Picture B-I Overview as the front door, and the other five Intelligence pages as the rooms it opens into.

[03:58 | Step 11 — FORECASTING group]
Last bucket, Forecasting. <break time="1.0s" /> Just one page this time. Ten under Analytics, five under Marketing, six under Intelligence, one here. That is your twenty-two, and now the whole sidebar fits in your head.

[04:16 | Step 12 — Forecasting]
Click Forecasting. <break time="1.4s" /> This is the only page that looks forward instead of back. Four numbers set the scene: next month's projected revenue, how close you are running against plan for the year, and how accurate the model has been. See the New Record button? We leave that one alone.

[04:38 | Step 13 — Forecasting panels and list]
Below, the workhorse. <break time="1.0s" /> A revenue projection, the forecast broken out by location, and an accuracy readout. Then the Forecast List itself: every period lined up with what you predicted, what actually came in, and the gap between the two.

[05:04 | Step 14 — Read a row]
Read one line and it clicks. <break time="1.4s" /> Take May of last year: forecast around two hundred thirty-eight thousand, actual came in near two forty-two, off by a point and a half. That is a closed month. The pending row shows dashes, because its real numbers are not in yet. Hands off New Record. We are only reading.

[05:32 | Step 15 — Recap]
So that is the map. <break time="0.8s" /> Four buckets. Analytics for the dashboards, Marketing for the reports, Intelligence for the deep questions, Forecasting for what comes next. Twenty-two pages, and you can reach any of them from the sidebar, or jump straight there with Search. Learn the buckets, and you never hunt for a page again.
