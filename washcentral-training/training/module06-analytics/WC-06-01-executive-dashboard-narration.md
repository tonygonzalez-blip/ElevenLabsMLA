### Narration craft standard (project-wide)

1. **The 1-second visual buffer.** Never speak the instant a screen changes. Each action cue is followed by `<break time="1.4s" />` so the click and transition land before the explanation begins.
2. **Varying sentence length.** Short fragments set against longer explanatory lines; no two adjacent blocks with the same rhythm.
3. **Show, don't read.** Narrate what a panel means for the technician's day, not the labels already on screen.

## Timed script

[00:00 | Step 1 — Arrive on Executive Dashboard]
This is the screen WashCentral opens to. <break time="1.4s" /> The Executive Dashboard is the whole network on one page. Before you ever open a ticket or a single site, this is where you check the pulse of the business. Learn to read it quickly, because a lot of support calls start with a number that lives right here.

[00:25 | Step 2 — The state of the business]
Read the band up top. <break time="1.2s" /> Network, April, month to date. Every card below rolls all your locations into one figure, so what you see is the business as a whole, not any single wash. The location selector in the header is what narrows it when you need one site.

[00:47 | Step 3 — Period toggles]
Now find the period row on the right. <break time="1.4s" /> Today, Week, Month, Quarter, Y-T-D, one year. It is set to Month. That one choice decides the window every card and panel reports on, so always check it before you trust a number.

[01:06 | Step 4 — Flip the period]
Click through the periods, one at a time. <break time="1.4s" /> Watch the numbers redraw for today, then the week, then the quarter, then the year. Nothing is edited. You are only changing the lens, and landing back on Month puts it right where it started.

[01:25 | Step 5 — Scale cards]
Start with the first three cards. <break time="1.4s" /> Revenue, cars washed, active members. These are your scale numbers, each with its change against the month before and a small trend line. Green arrows mean the network is growing, and growth in members is the one that quietly lifts the other two.

[01:47 | Step 6 — Health cards]
Now the next three. <break time="1.4s" /> Conversion rate, visit frequency, and labor percent. Labor is the one to watch. It has gone amber because it is running over the fifteen percent target, and that color is the dashboard flagging it for you. When you see amber, that is your cue to look at which site is pulling it up.

[02:13 | Step 7 — Revenue trend]
Drop to the big panel. <break time="1.2s" /> Twelve months of revenue, stacked by where it came from: memberships, pay per wash, and retail. The membership band is the thick one at the base. That is the recurring money you want climbing, month after month.

[02:32 | Step 8 — Location health]
On the right, every site in one list. <break time="1.4s" /> Revenue, cars, labor, and how close each runs to its conversion target. The dots and the numbers slide from green toward red as a site slips. One look tells you Naples Coast is the one to check on today, and it saves you scanning six separate reports to find it.

[02:57 | Step 9 — Members vs churn]
Keep going to the members panel. <break time="1.2s" /> A line for how many members you have, against bars for the ones you gained and the ones you lost, month by month. The gap between those bars is what grows or shrinks the base over time.

[03:17 | Step 10 — Revenue mix]
Beside it, the revenue mix. <break time="1.2s" /> A single donut for how this month's money splits apart. It is the quick shape of where revenue is coming from right now, before you go digging for detail.

[03:33 | Step 11 — Drill into Revenue]
Click the Revenue card. <break time="1.4s" /> It opens a page just for revenue: today's take, the average ticket, the member share, add-ons, and a table by location. So the cards are not dead ends. Any one of them is a door to the next level of detail.

_[Director: click the Revenue card to open Revenue Detail; do not click Open Reports or the Jump to workflow links.]_

[03:53 | Step 12 — Back to the dashboard]
Click Back to Executive Dashboard. <break time="1.2s" /> You return to the full scorecard, still on Month, exactly as you left it.

[04:02 | Step 13 — Recap]
That is the whole dashboard. <break time="0.8s" /> Pick a period, read the six cards for scale and health, then the panels for the trend, the sites, your members, and the mix. It edits nothing, and every card can take you deeper. Start your day here, then go where the numbers point.
