# WC-12-08 · Customer Portal & AI Agent (Settings)

v1.0 · July 9, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 9, 2026.**

## Purpose

This lesson tours two Settings areas that a customer touches directly: the **Customer Portal** they log into, and the **AI Agent** that answers them on chat, text, and phone. It comes in two parts. Part 1 walks the portal hub and its four configuration subpages. Part 2 walks the AI Agent overview and its subpages. By the end you can find any portal or agent setting, read what it is set to, and know which two settings to check when a customer cannot log in and when the agent hands a conversation to a person. Every screen here is read-only for support: you never **Publish Changes** and you never send a test message.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians who are already comfortable moving around Settings. You should be signed in and inside **Settings** (from any page, open the left rail). No prior work on the portal or the agent is assumed. The session timeout ("Still there?") prompt can appear on any long-lived screen; it is covered in WC-01-03 and is out of scope here.

## Navigation path

Both areas live in the Settings left rail. The portal is under `Settings → Customer Portal▾ → Portal Overview`, with subpages **Branding & Appearance**, **Features & Modules**, **Access & Security**, and **Content & Messaging**. The agent is under `Settings → AI Agent▾ → Overview`, with subpages **Configuration**, **Knowledge Base**, **Channels & Deployment**, **Conversations**, **Escalations**, and **Analytics**.

## Steps

One action per step. Hold each new screen for two to three seconds before moving on. Steps 1 through 9 are Part 1 (Customer Portal). Steps 10 through 20 are Part 2 (AI Agent).

You can open the **Customer Portal** hub, read its KPI band and four configuration cards, and step into **Branding & Appearance**, **Features & Modules**, **Access & Security**, and **Content & Messaging** to see how each is set. ## Notes and troubleshooting (observed behavior only)

- The portal is one consolidated hub with four subpages, not the larger page set some legacy material describes. The five surfaces are **Portal Overview**, **Branding & Appearance**, **Features & Modules**, **Access & Security**, and **Content & Messaging** (a separate **Portals View** lists portals). See the discrepancy note below.
- Module count mismatch: the hub **Features & Modules** card reads **ENABLED MODULES 7 of 9**, while the **Features & Modules** subpage tile reads **Modules Enabled 9 / 10** ("9 of 10 modules enabled"). The two pages disagree on both the enabled count and the total. Read the number off the subpage when you need the authoritative value, and flag the mismatch.
- The portal subpages are edit forms with **Save Changes** or **Save & Publish**, and **Features & Modules** adds **Enable All**, **Disable All**, and **Reset to Defaults**. None of these are clicked in this lesson. **Publish Changes** on the hub is the button that pushes edits live to customers; support never uses it.
- On the AI Agent side, three subpages render against a different, empty demo tenant than the Overview. **Overview**, **Channels & Deployment**, **Escalations**, and **Analytics** show the live **Atlas Car Wash** tenant with populated data. **Configuration** shows **Express Wash Concepts Demo** as an unsaved form ("Not yet saved"), and **Knowledge Base** and **Conversations** show an empty tenant (**0 Articles**, **No conversations found**). Document the structure of those three pages from what is on screen; the live saved values for the running agent are not observable in this capture and are parked.
- **Channels & Deployment** carries send-type controls (**Send Test Message**, **Send Test SMS**, **Place Test Call**) and **Escalations** carries **Claim Next**, which assigns a live record. None of these are exercised here. Whether technicians may run test prompts against the agent in the demo tenant is parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #8).
- The **Preview Portal** button opens the customer-facing portal for a read-only look and can be closed without publishing. The portal's rendered customer screens are not documented in this lesson.
- The session timeout ("Still there?") prompt can surface on any of these screens after a period of inactivity; it is not part of this lesson. See WC-01-03.
