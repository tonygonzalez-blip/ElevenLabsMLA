# WC-09-01 · Portal pages (Digital)

v2.0 · July 11, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 11, 2026.** (Portal Members verified July 9; the four sibling pages verified July 11 via in-app navigation.)

## Purpose

This lesson walks the **CUSTOMER PORTAL** group inside the **Digital** module: the pages that back the customer-facing portal your washes' members log in to. You will learn where the group lives, read **Portal Members** end to end (its KPIs, its three health cards, and its member roster columns), and open the four service entries that sit beside it: **Booking Page (Customer)**, **Portal Messages**, **Portal Billing**, and **Portal Notifications**. You open pages and read them; you never send a message or a notification, you never edit a member, and you never complete a booking.

One route note to carry with you: all five pages in the group load through in-app sidebar navigation. On July 9 the four sibling pages 404'd when probed by direct URL; on July 11 a credentialed pass reached every one of them through the sidebar, and this guide now documents what each shows. If a direct URL fails you, the sidebar is the front door.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians. You should be signed in and comfortable with the global header (WC-01-01) and with WashCentral's list layout, KPIs, and filters (WC-01-04). No portal-admin experience is assumed. Session-timeout behavior (the "Still there?" prompt) is out of scope here; see WC-01-03.

## Navigation path

`Digital → CUSTOMER PORTAL → Portal Members` for the one list this lesson reads in full. Open the **Digital** module on the left rail; the sidebar then shows two groups, **CUSTOMER PORTAL** on top and **MOBILE** below it. The five entries this lesson covers all sit in **CUSTOMER PORTAL**: **Booking Page (Customer)**, **Portal Members**, **Portal Messages**, **Portal Billing**, and **Portal Notifications**. The consolidation of portal and mobile surfaces under one **Digital** module is the current platform layout (see DISCREPANCIES.md #16).

## Steps

One action per step. Hold each new screen for two to three seconds before moving on. Steps 8 through 11 open the four sibling pages read-only; nothing in the group is sent, edited, booked, or launched.

1. On the left rail, open the **Digital** module and look at the sidebar. **Expected:** the sidebar header reads **Digital**, and the pages split into two groups. The top group, **CUSTOMER PORTAL**, lists five entries: **Booking Page (Customer)**, **Portal Members**, **Portal Messages**, **Portal Billing**, and **Portal Notifications**. Below it, a **MOBILE** group holds the mobile-app pages, which are a separate lesson (WC-09-02).
2. Click **Portal Members**. **Expected:** the page opens with the eyebrow **CUSTOMER PORTAL** above the title **Portal Members**, and the breadcrumb reads `Customer Portal › Portal Members`. This is the roster of everyone who holds a portal login.
3. Read the KPI strip across the top. **Expected:** four figures sit in a row: **Registered** (1,840), **Active This Month** (1,240), **New This Week** (42), and **Mobile Users** (68%). Together they answer "how big is the portal audience, and how much of it is live right now."
4. Read the three insight cards below the KPIs. **Expected:** **Member Growth** (subtitle "Last 12 months") trends the registered count over the year. **By Plan** (subtitle "Plan distribution") breaks the base down by plan, with Monthly Unlimited the largest share, then Annual Plan, Monthly Basic, and Trial. **Member Status** (subtitle "Account health") splits the base into Active, Inactive, and Suspended. These are the same shapes you will meet on other WashCentral list pages: a KPI strip, then a few summary cards, then the list.
5. Read the **Member List** card header and its toolbar. **Expected:** the card is labelled **PORTAL · MEMBERS** with the title **Member List** and a record count ("4 records"). On the right sit **+ New Record** and **Export**. Below them is a search box with the placeholder "Search portal members…", a **Filters ▾** control, and a **Search** button. In this lesson you locate these controls; you do not create a record and you do not export.
6. Read the Member List column headers. **Expected:** six columns run left to right: **NAME**, **EMAIL**, **MEMBER SINCE**, **PLAN**, **PORTAL STATUS**, and **LAST LOGIN**. **PORTAL STATUS** is the one to check first on a "customer cannot log in to the portal" call; the four sample rows all read Active, and the **Member Status** card above splits the wider base into Active, Inactive, and Suspended.
7. Read the pagination row under the list. **Expected:** the footer shows **ROWS PER PAGE** with options 25, 50, and 100, a counter reading "1–4 of 4", and a pager (`‹ 1 ›`). Reading the list, paging it, and searching it change nothing. **+ New Record** would open a create form; that form did not resolve in the July 9 corpus, so its layout is TBD — parked (new parked item; see Notes). **Export** is a data-out action and is not clicked in this lesson.
8. Click **Booking Page (Customer)** in the **CUSTOMER PORTAL** group. **Expected:** the page opens as the customer's view, not an admin list — it drops the WashCentral chrome entirely and shows the branded booking site (page title **Book a Service**, header **atlas car wash** with **Book a service**, **Help**, and **Sign in** links). A five-step wizard runs across the top: **1 Service**, **2 Options**, **3 Location**, **4 Date & Time**, **5 Confirm**. Step 1 asks "What can we help you with?" over three service-category cards: **State Inspection** (annual safety + emissions, "3 options · from 30 min"), **Oil Change** ("3 options · from 30 min"), and **Five Star Detailing** ("3 options · from 1 hr 30 min"), with a **Continue →** button. Read the first screen and leave; never advance the wizard toward a real booking. Return to the admin sidebar to continue. (The "atlas car wash" branding is a demo-tenant oddity; see Notes.)
9. Click **Portal Messages**. Do not open a row, compose, or reply. **Expected:** the portal-side message inbox loads (breadcrumb **Customer Portal › Portal Messages**). KPIs: **14 Unread**, **28 Open**, **3.2 hrs Avg Response**, **4.6/5 Satisfaction**, over insight cards **Message Volume** (Last 12 months, 342, ↑ +8%), **By Status**, and **Response Health**. The **Message List** columns run **DATE, CUSTOMER, SUBJECT, STATUS, PRIORITY, ASSIGNED TO** — rows like "Billing question for May" (Open, Medium) and "Membership cancellation request" (In Progress, High). This is where member messages land and get worked; replying reaches a real customer, so in training you read the list and touch nothing.
10. Click **Portal Billing**. **Expected:** the portal-side billing activity log loads (breadcrumb **Customer Portal › Portal Billing**). KPIs: **8 Updates Today**, **4 Failed Billing**, **2 Pending Review**, **148 Auto-Renewed**, over cards **Billing Activity**, **By Action**, and **Billing Health** (84% SUCCESS / 10% PENDING / 6% FAILED). The **Billing Records** columns run **DATE, CUSTOMER, ACTION, PLAN, AMOUNT, STATUS** — card updates, plan upgrades, auto-renewals, and failures such as "Card Failed / Monthly Basic / $19.99 / Failed". On a "my card was declined" call, **ACTION** and **STATUS** here tell the story. Read-only, always: billing rows are live money.
11. Click **Portal Notifications**. Do not send, create, or edit a notification. **Expected:** the portal-side automated-notification list loads (breadcrumb **Customer Portal › Portal Notifications**). KPIs: **12 Active Notifications**, **840 Sent Today**, **38% Avg Open Rate**, **12% Clicks**. The **Notification List** columns run **NAME, TYPE, TRIGGER, AUDIENCE, SENT, OPEN RATE, STATUS** — rows such as **Welcome Email** (Email, Account Created, New Members) and **Renewal Reminder** (Email/SMS, 7 Days Before, All Members). These are live sends to real members, so this page is looked at and never touched in training.
12. Recap the group. **Expected:** the **CUSTOMER PORTAL** group holds five entries, and you have now read all five: **Portal Members** for the roster and login questions, **Booking Page (Customer)** for what the customer actually sees when booking, **Portal Messages** for inbound member messages, **Portal Billing** for the billing activity log, and **Portal Notifications** for the automated sends. All of it reached from the sidebar (direct URLs 404), and nothing sent, edited, booked, or exported during the walk.

## Expected results

You can open the **Digital** module, find the **CUSTOMER PORTAL** group, and read all five of its pages. On Portal Members you can read the four KPIs, the three health cards, and the six roster columns, and you know **PORTAL STATUS** is the field to check when a member reports a portal-login problem. You can open the customer's booking wizard and describe its five steps without advancing it, read the message inbox and its status and priority columns, read the billing activity log for a declined-card call, and read the automated-notification list. Reading these pages changes nothing, and no message, notification, booking, export, or edit happens during the walk.

## Notes and troubleshooting (observed behavior only)

- **In-app navigation only.** The four sibling pages (`portal-book.html`, `portal-messages.html`, `portal-billing.html`, `portal-notifications.html`) 404'd on direct URL on July 9 and loaded normally through the in-app sidebar on July 11 (the app routes them itself). Steps 8–11 are written from that July 11 credentialed capture, clearing the July 9 parked marker for the four pages' contents.
- **Booking Page (Customer) leaves the admin chrome.** It renders the customer-facing site — branded **atlas car wash**, with **Sign in** and **Help** links and the five-step booking wizard — rather than a WashCentral list page. The "Atlas Car Wash" branding does not match this tenant's location names (MLAS, SSNB, Piscataway) and is reported as a new discrepancy (demo branding artifact). Never advance the wizard past reading step 1; a completed booking is a real record.
- **Portal Messages** and **Portal Notifications** are send-type surfaces. You may open and read the list pages (that is what steps 9 and 11 do); you never open a compose, reply, or send flow, and no message or notification is depicted leaving the platform at any point.
- **Portal Billing rows are live money.** The page is an activity log (card updates, renewals, failures), and it is read-only in every training context.
- The **Portal Members** create/edit form (`portal-members-edit.html`) was not captured on July 9 or July 11, so the **+ New Record** form layout remains TBD — parked.
- **Export** (present on Portal Members, Portal Messages, Portal Billing, and Portal Notifications) is a data-out action and is not clicked during this lesson.
- **Narration status.** The v1.0 narration voices blocks 8, 10, 11, and 12 around the July 9 404 state ("the page itself didn't open… contents stay parked"), and block 9 narrates Portal Messages as an entry we locate without opening. All five of those blocks contradict the v2.0 steps and need re-author and re-render before recording; see the narration file's status header.
- If the idle "Still there?" prompt appears while you are on a portal page, it belongs to session-timeout behavior, not this lesson. Dismiss it and continue; see WC-01-03.

**Knowledge check [READ-ONLY].** (1) Which page in the **CUSTOMER PORTAL** group carries the member roster, and which column do you check first for a "cannot log in to the portal" call? (2) Name the four KPIs on **Portal Members**. (3) A member says their card was declined and asks why their plan lapsed; which page in this group shows that event, and which two columns tell you what happened? (4) You paste `portal-billing.html` into the browser and get a 404; is the page gone? (Answers: (1) **Portal Members**; **PORTAL STATUS**. (2) **Registered**, **Active This Month**, **New This Week**, **Mobile Users**. (3) **Portal Billing**; **ACTION** and **STATUS**. (4) No — these pages load through in-app sidebar navigation only.)
