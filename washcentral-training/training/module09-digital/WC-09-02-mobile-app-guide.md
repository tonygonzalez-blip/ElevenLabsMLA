# WC-09-02 · Mobile app pages (Digital)

v1.0 · July 9, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 9, 2026.**

## Purpose

This lesson walks the **MOBILE** group inside the **Digital** module: the pages behind the customer mobile app. You will read **Mobile App Users** end to end, the one page a technician opens on a live call, so that when a customer says the app is misbehaving you can pull their record and check their **APP VERSION** and **NOTIFICATIONS** state in seconds. You will then open the three configuration pages that theme and stock the app, **Mobile App Branding**, **Mobile App Features**, and **Push Notifications**, and learn to recognize each without touching it. This is a read-only tour. Nothing on any page is saved, published, or sent, and no push notification is composed or delivered.

One expectation to set up front: on the July 9 verification pass, **Mobile App Users** resolved to a live page and the three configuration pages resolved in the **Settings** area. Two things the plan mentions did not resolve in the corpus, so they are parked rather than guessed: the **Mobile Feedback** page, and a standalone customer app-preview launcher. Where the app preview is concerned, each configuration page carries its own in-page preview, which is what you use instead.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians. You should be signed in and comfortable with the global header (WC-01-01) and with WashCentral's list layout, KPIs, and filters (WC-01-04). No mobile-app-admin experience is assumed. Session-timeout behavior (the "Still there?" prompt) is out of scope here; see WC-01-03. Ask Shyne, the assistant in the header, is covered separately in WC-01-02.

## Navigation path

`Digital → MOBILE → Mobile App Users` for the one list this lesson reads in full. Open the **Digital** module on the left rail; the sidebar then shows two groups, **CUSTOMER PORTAL** on top and **MOBILE** below it. The five entries this lesson covers all sit in **MOBILE**: **Mobile App Branding**, **Mobile App Features**, **Mobile App Users**, **Push Notifications**, and **Mobile Feedback**. Opening **Mobile App Branding**, **Mobile App Features**, or **Push Notifications** switches the shell to the **Settings** area (the left rail changes to the Settings menu, with **Close Settings** and **All Settings** at the top). The consolidation of portal and mobile surfaces under one **Digital** module is the current platform layout (see DISCREPANCIES.md #16).

## Steps

One action per step. Hold each new screen for two to three seconds before moving on. Every page in this lesson is read only; nothing is saved, published, sent, or exported.

1. On the left rail, open the **Digital** module and stay on **Mobile App Users**. Look at the **MOBILE** group in the sidebar. **Expected:** the **MOBILE** group lists five entries: **Mobile App Branding**, **Mobile App Features**, **Mobile App Users**, **Push Notifications**, and **Mobile Feedback**. The header shows the eyebrow **MOBILE** above the title **Mobile App Users**. This is the technician's window into who runs the app.
2. Read the KPI band across the top. **Expected:** four tiles sit in a row: **Total Users** (4,820), **Active Monthly** (3,240), **iOS** (58%), and **Android** (42%). The split between **iOS** and **Android** tells you, before you ask, which platform most of your callers are on.
3. Read the three insight cards below the KPIs. **Expected:** **User Growth** (subtitle "Last 12 months") trends the user count over the year and shows +31%. **By Platform** (subtitle "Device distribution") breaks users down by device: iOS 58%, Android 42%, Tablet 0%, Other 0%. **User Status** (subtitle "Activity overview") splits the base into 67% active, 21% monthly, and 12% inactive. A growing inactive slice is worth a second look.
4. In the **Mobile User List**, read the column headers. **Expected:** the card is labelled **MOBILE · USERS** with the title **Mobile User List** and a record count ("4 records"). Seven columns run left to right: **NAME · EMAIL · APP VERSION · PLATFORM · LAST ACTIVE · NOTIFICATIONS · STATUS**. Two of those seven do most of the troubleshooting work.
5. Read one row across as if it were a support ticket. **[READ-ONLY]** **Expected:** each row carries a customer's **APP VERSION**, **PLATFORM**, **LAST ACTIVE** date, and **NOTIFICATIONS** state, either On or Off. When a customer says the app is broken, this row is your first stop: an old **APP VERSION** explains stale behavior, and **NOTIFICATIONS** set to Off explains why they never see a push. In the sample data, app versions run from 4.0.5 up to 4.2.1 across both **iOS** and **Android**, and one row shows **NOTIFICATIONS** Off while the rest show On.
6. Look at the list toolbar, and do not click it. **Expected:** above the table sit **+ New Record** and **Export**, plus a search box with the placeholder "Search mobile app users…", a **Filters ▾** control, and a **Search** button. You would search here by name or email to pull one customer's app record. The pager below reads "1–4 of 4" with a **25** rows-per-page control. **Export** and **+ New Record** are not used in this lesson.
7. From the **MOBILE** group, open **Mobile App Branding**. **Expected:** the branding page opens in the **Settings** area (the left rail switches to the Settings menu, with **Close Settings** and **All Settings** at the top). The page is titled **Mobile App Branding**, tagged **Mobile**, with a **● Loaded** status. Four sections stack down the page, **Tenant**, **Brand**, **Colors**, and **Support**, and a **LIVE PREVIEW** phone on the right renders the branded app.
8. Read the **Brand**, **Colors**, and **Support** fields. Do not click **Save & Publish**. **Expected:** **Brand** holds **Display name**, **Tagline**, **Logo URL (light)**, **Logo URL (dark)**, and **Hero image URL**. **Colors** holds **Primary**, **Primary (dark)**, **Hero gradient (3 stops)**, and **Accent**. **Support** holds **Support phone**, **Support email**, and **FAQ URL**. The **Tenant** panel at the top selects which brand you are theming. Editing any field and publishing would re-theme the live app, so this page is read only here.
9. From the **MOBILE** group, open **Mobile App Features**. Do not touch a toggle and do not click **Save & Publish**. **Expected:** the page is titled **Mobile App Features & Games**, tagged **Mobile**. The **Games** section lists six on/off modules: **Daily Spin**, **Wash Streak**, **Lucky Wash**, **Scratch Cards**, **Slot Machine**, and **Wash Bingo**. Below it, **Wallet & Gift Cards** lists **Wallet** and **Gift Cards**. Each row has a switch, and the **LIVE PREVIEW** reflects whatever is turned on. These are per-brand feature flags, so flipping one changes what the real app offers.
10. From the **MOBILE** group, open **Push Notifications**. Never compose, publish, or send anything on this page. **Expected:** the page is titled **Push Campaigns**, tagged **Mobile**. It has three sections, **Tenant**, **Campaigns**, and **Editor**, with a **LOCK-SCREEN PREVIEW** and an **ESTIMATED REACH** panel on the right. The demo tenant reads "No campaigns yet." Composing and sending push is marketing work, not a technician task, so **+ New campaign** and **Save & Publish** are not clicked.
11. Look at the preview panels on the configuration pages. **Expected:** each page carries a preview of what the customer sees: a **LIVE PREVIEW** phone on **Mobile App Branding** and **Mobile App Features**, and a **LOCK-SCREEN PREVIEW** on **Push Campaigns**. These panels are how you check the customer's view from inside the console. A separate standalone customer app-preview launcher is not present in this build: TBD — parked (new parked item; see Notes).
12. Click **Close Settings** to leave the configuration area. **Expected:** the Settings menu closes and you return to the **Digital** module with **Mobile App Users** and the **MOBILE** group visible again. Nothing was saved, published, or sent.
13. Point to **Mobile Feedback** in the **MOBILE** group. Do not click it. **Expected:** **Mobile Feedback** is confirmed as an entry in the group, but its page did not load in the July 9 capture: TBD — parked (new parked item; see Notes). Its sidebar entry is documented; its contents are not, so treat them as unverified until walked live.
14. Recap the group. **Expected:** the **MOBILE** group holds five entries. **Mobile App Users** is the one that reads in full today: a KPI band, three insight cards, and a seven-column list you check for app-trouble calls. **Mobile App Branding**, **Mobile App Features**, and **Push Notifications** are configuration pages you recognize but leave alone. **Mobile Feedback** and the standalone app-preview launcher are parked. Nothing in the group is saved, published, sent, or exported during this walk.

## Expected results

You can open the **Digital** module, find the **MOBILE** group, and read **Mobile App Users** in full: its four KPIs (**Total Users**, **Active Monthly**, **iOS**, **Android**), its three insight cards (**User Growth**, **By Platform**, **User Status**), and its seven-column list. You know **APP VERSION** and **NOTIFICATIONS** are the two columns to read first when a customer reports an app problem. You can open **Mobile App Branding**, **Mobile App Features**, and **Push Notifications**, name what each configures, and use each page's in-page preview to see the customer's view, all without changing a field. You can point out **Mobile Feedback** and state honestly that its contents are parked. Reading these pages changes nothing, and no publish, toggle, campaign, send, or export happens during the walk.

## Notes and troubleshooting (observed behavior only)

- **First stop for an "app not working" call:** open **Mobile App Users**, find the customer, and read **APP VERSION** and **NOTIFICATIONS**. A stale **APP VERSION** points to an app that needs updating; **NOTIFICATIONS** set to Off explains a customer who never receives push.
- **The three configuration pages are read-only reference for a technician.** **Mobile App Branding** (colors, logos, and copy), **Mobile App Features** (game and wallet flags), and **Push Notifications** (**Push Campaigns**) all render in the **Settings** area and all carry a **Save & Publish** button that a technician does not use. Publishing branding or flipping a feature flag changes the live customer app.
- **Push is never sent from this lesson.** On **Push Campaigns**, **+ New campaign** and **Save & Publish** are left untouched, and no message is composed or delivered. The demo tenant showed "No campaigns yet." on the July 9 pass.
- **Previews are in-page.** Each configuration page shows the customer's view beside its settings: a **LIVE PREVIEW** phone on Branding and Features, a **LOCK-SCREEN PREVIEW** on Push Campaigns. A separate standalone customer app-preview launcher (described in the plan as an "Open Mobile App Preview") did not appear in the corpus and is TBD — parked as a NEW parked item.
- **Mobile Feedback** has a confirmed sidebar entry, but its standalone page did not resolve in the July 9 corpus. Its KPIs and columns are TBD — parked as a NEW parked item; the plan's description of it is orientation only and is not presented here as observed.
- **Related Settings surface:** mobile dashboard content (promo banner, card rail, announcements) is set on a separate **Mobile Content Blocks** page under Marketing settings. It sits outside the **MOBILE** group taught here and is not part of this lesson.
- If the idle "Still there?" prompt appears on any of these pages, it belongs to session-timeout behavior, not this lesson. Dismiss it and continue; see WC-01-03.

**Knowledge check [READ-ONLY].** (1) A customer calls to say the app never alerts them. Which page do you open, and which two columns tell you why? (2) Name the four KPIs on **Mobile App Users**. (3) Which page would you use to change the app's colors and logo, and why does a technician not click **Save & Publish** on it? (Answers: (1) **Mobile App Users**; **APP VERSION** and **NOTIFICATIONS**. (2) **Total Users**, **Active Monthly**, **iOS**, **Android**. (3) **Mobile App Branding**; publishing there re-themes the live customer app, which is not a technician task.)
