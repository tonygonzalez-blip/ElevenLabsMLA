### Narration craft standard (project-wide)

1. **The 1-second visual buffer.** Never speak the instant a screen changes. Each action cue is followed by `<break time="1.4s" />` so the click and transition land before the explanation begins.
2. **Varying sentence length.** Short fragments set against longer explanatory lines; no two adjacent blocks with the same rhythm.
3. **Show, don't read.** Narrate what a page means for the technician's day, not the labels already on screen.

## Timed script

[00:00 | Step 1 — MOBILE group]
Open Digital, and stay on Mobile App Users. <break time="1.0s" /> The sidebar splits the customer's world in two: the portal, and the mobile app. Under Mobile you get five pages. This lesson visits all of them, but you only read. Nothing here gets saved.

[00:20 | Step 2 — KPI band]
Four numbers run across the top. <break time="1.4s" /> Total users, active this month, and the split between iOS and Android. That last pair earns its keep on a support call. It tells you, before you ask, which phone your customer is probably holding.

[00:41 | Step 3 — Insight cards]
Three cards fill in the shape. <break time="1.0s" /> One tracks growth over the year. One breaks the base down by device. The last sorts users into active, monthly, and gone quiet. A rising inactive slice is worth a second look.

[00:59 | Step 4 — List columns]
Now the list itself. <break time="1.0s" /> Seven columns per user: name, email, app version, platform, last active, notifications, and status. Two of those seven do most of the troubleshooting work.

[01:13 | Step 5 — Troubleshooting]
Read one row like a support ticket. <break time="1.4s" /> App version tells you whether they're stuck behind a fix. Notifications, on or off, tells you why a customer swears the alert never came. Start here, before you touch anything else.

_[Director: point across the row; do not open it. Demo names and emails stay on screen but are not read aloud.]_

[01:33 | Step 6 — List toolbar]
Above the table, the usual controls. <break time="1.0s" /> Search pulls up one customer by name or email. There's also Export and a New Record button. Leave both alone today; reading the list is all a technician needs.

_[Director: do not click Export or + New Record.]_

[01:49 | Step 7 — Open Mobile App Branding]
Now the pages that build the app. Open Mobile App Branding. <break time="1.4s" /> Notice the shell flips to Settings. This is where a brand's name, logo, colors, and support details get set, with a live phone preview alongside.

[02:08 | Step 8 — Branding fields]
Four blocks, top to bottom. <break time="1.0s" /> Tenant, brand, colors, support. Display names and taglines, logo links, the primary and accent colors, a support phone and email. Editing any of it and publishing would re-skin the real app, so we only look.

_[Director: do not click Save & Publish; edit no field.]_

[02:28 | Step 9 — Mobile App Features]
Back to the group, open Mobile App Features. <break time="1.4s" /> These are on-and-off switches for the fun: daily spin, wash streak, scratch cards, a few more, plus wallet and gift cards. Flip one and the preview updates. We flip nothing.

_[Director: do not touch a toggle; do not click Save & Publish.]_

[02:48 | Step 10 — Push Notifications]
Last page, Push Notifications. <break time="1.4s" /> It opens as Push Campaigns: write a message, pick an audience, watch it land on a lock-screen preview. That's marketing's job, not ours. We create no campaign here, and we send nothing.
