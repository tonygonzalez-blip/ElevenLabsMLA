# WC-00-01 · Workstation Prerequisites (Setup)

v1.0 · July 9, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 9, 2026.**

> Doc-only lesson. There is no video and no narration for this one. It is a written checklist you complete once, before the rest of the program.

## Purpose

Get your workstation ready for two things: doing support work in WashCentral, and recording clean training material when it is your turn to produce a lesson. WashCentral is a web application, so there is nothing to install. The setup here is about the browser, the display, and the access you need before you sign in.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians starting the training program: Tech Support, Pre-Configuration and Onboarding, and Field Techs. No prior WashCentral experience is assumed. You do need a work computer with a modern desktop browser and the account details your administrator issues.

## What you need

WashCentral runs entirely in the browser. There is no desktop client, no plugin, and no local database. A current version of Chrome, Edge, Firefox, or Safari on a desktop or laptop is enough for daily support work.

For recording training material, the requirements are stricter because every lesson in this program ships at a fixed size and quality:

- A display that can show the browser at **1920×1080** with no scaling.
- Browser zoom at **100%**.
- A clean browser profile with the bookmarks bar hidden and no extension badges in view.
- Operating-system notifications silenced (Do Not Disturb or Focus) so nothing pops in mid-recording.

These match the project recording standard used for every finished lesson. If you are only consuming training, you can skip the recording-specific items; if you will produce lessons, treat them as required.

## Access to line up before you start

Ask your administrator or team lead for the following, so you are not blocked partway through:

- **Demo tenant credentials** for `demo.washcentral.com`: an email or username, a password, and a **Tenant / Customer Code**. All three are issued to you. Signing in is covered in WC-00-02.
- **The in-platform Knowledge Base**, which you reach from inside WashCentral once you are signed in (covered in Module 10).
- **Jira CSI board access**, if your role handles migration switches. *Migration note (source: Migration FAQ):* the beta-to-live switch for a client moving off WashAssist is requested through a Jira ticket on the CSI board, so migration-facing techs need that access.
- **Zoho Desk access**, only if your team uses the Help Desk integration (covered in Module 10). Teams that do not use Zoho can ignore this.

## Expected result

When this lesson is done you have a browser that opens `demo.washcentral.com` cleanly, a display and profile that meet the recording standard if you will be producing lessons, and the three pieces of access above requested or in hand. You have not signed in yet. That is the next lesson.

## Notes and troubleshooting

- Nothing about WashCentral installs to your machine. If someone tells you to install a WashCentral client, that is a red flag: the platform is web-only.
- The 1920×1080 requirement is about the finished video matching every other lesson, not about how the platform looks day to day. Support work is fine at any reasonable resolution.
- If you do not have a Tenant / Customer Code, stop and get it before WC-00-02. Sign-in needs all three fields, and there is no way around the tenant code.
- Screen recording, cursor overlays, and the capture pipeline are handled per the project recording standard when you produce a lesson; you do not set those up as part of daily support.
