# WC-03-03 · The rest of the Transactions menu (Sales)

v1.1 · July 11, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 11, 2026.**

## Purpose

The **Sales** module's sidebar holds nine pages under the header **TRANSACTIONS** (as of July 11 — see the drift note in step 1). Lessons WC-03-01 and WC-03-02 cover the first one, **Tickets**, and its Ticket View; the new **Refund Approvals** page is not yet captured and sits outside this tour. This lesson walks the seven list pages so you know, at a glance, which page answers which support question. By the end you can open **Ticket Details**, **Payments**, **Payouts**, **Shifts**, **Declined Credit Card Log**, **Gift Card Transactions**, and **House Account Transactions**, and read each one's KPIs and columns. Every page is a read-only list you filter and search; none of them change data on open.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians who have finished WC-03-01 (Tickets list) and WC-03-02 (Ticket View). You should be signed in and able to reach the **Sales** module from the left rail. This is a menu tour: it names each page and what it is for, not a deep dive into any single one. Payment declines and shift reconciliation get their own playbooks (PB-4 and PB-5).

## Navigation path

The Transactions sidebar sits under the **Sales** module in the left navigation; the page header reads **TRANSACTIONS**. All seven pages in this lesson are entries in that sidebar, so from any one of them you reach the next with a single click. Shorthand used below: `Sales → Payments`, `Sales → Payouts`, and so on.

## Steps

One action per step. The July 11 credentialed re-capture caught these pages healthy: live rows and populated headline KPIs everywhere except **House Account Transactions**, which was still loading at capture. (The July 9 sweep had caught them all in the **Couldn't load records (Failed to fetch)** error state; if you see that live, it is the record-loading service, not the page.) Hold each new screen for two to three seconds before moving on.

1. Open **Sales → Ticket Details** and look at the left sidebar. **Expected:** nine entries sit under the **TRANSACTIONS** header in this order: **Tickets**, **Ticket Details**, **Payments**, **Refund Approvals**, **Payouts**, **Shifts**, **Declined Credit Card Log**, **Gift Card Transactions**, **House Account Transactions**. **Tickets** and the Ticket View are WC-03-01 and WC-03-02; this lesson tours the seven list pages below. **Drift note (July 11):** **Refund Approvals** is new since the July 9 capture; its page is not yet captured and is not part of this tour (flagged for the curriculum map and DISCREPANCIES.md).

2. Stay on **Ticket Details** and read the page. **Expected:** the breadcrumb reads **Transactions › Ticket Details**. The KPI strip shows **Total Line Items** (193,600,035 on July 11), **Services (30d)**, **Discounts (30d)**, **Revenue (30d)**, with three cards beneath: **Line Item Trend**, **By Type**, **Revenue Health**. The search card **SEARCH TICKET DETAILS** has **Filters** and **Search**. The list **Ticket Detail List** (**193,600,035 RECORDS** on July 11) carries an **Export** button and twelve columns: **LOCATION · TICKET NO · DATE IN · TYPE · CATEGORY · PROFIT CENTER · SERVICE GROUP · DESCRIPTION · TOTAL · TAX · TAXABLE · CASHIER**. (**CATEGORY**, **PROFIT CENTER**, **SERVICE GROUP**, and **TAX** are new since the July 9 capture — drift, see notes.) Ticket Details is the line-item view: one row per line rung, not one row per ticket. Open it when you need to see the individual services, discounts, and adjustments on a sale rather than the ticket total.

3. Click **Payments**. **Expected:** **Sales Payments** loads. KPIs: **Total Payments** (112,151,868 on July 11), **Top Method (30d)**, **2nd Method (30d)**, **Volume (30d)**. Cards: **Payment Volume**, **By Method** (on July 11 it read "Not available at this data volume."), **Payment Health**. Search card **SEARCH PAYMENTS** with **Filters** and **Search**. The list **Payment List** (**112,151,868 RECORDS** on July 11) has **Export** and columns **TIME · LOCATION · TICKET # · METHOD · AMOUNT**. A July 11 example row: **2026-01-11 03:57 · 616 - Bridgeville Millers Run Road · WA90919202691 · CREDIT CARD · $34.24**. This is every completed payment across the network. The **Payment Health** card states plainly that approval and decline status are not tracked here, because Payments records completed transactions only. Declined attempts live on their own page (step 6). Search by ticket number to see how one ticket was tendered.

4. Click **Payouts**. **Expected:** **Payouts** loads. KPIs: **Total Payouts** (30,750 on July 11) and **Volume (30d)** ($6,384.99), with the two middle tiles naming the top vendors for the window — on July 11 they read **Petty Cash (30d)** 44% and **Misc. (30d)** 39% (in the July 9 error-state capture the labels showed generically as **Top Vendor (30d)** / **2nd Vendor (30d)**; the tile labels take the vendor names once data loads). Cards: **Payout Trend**, **By Vendor**, **Payout Status**. Search card **SEARCH PAYOUTS** with **Filters ▾** and **Search**. The list **Payout List** (**30,750 RECORDS**) has **+ New Record**, **Export**, and columns **LOCATION · PAYOUT ID · SHIFT # · CASHIER · TERMINAL · VENDOR · AMOUNT**. A July 11 example row: **110 - Heath Hebron · PYT-32757 · 35623 · Noah Murphy · POS · Misc. · $5.00**. Payouts is cash paid out of the register, such as petty cash or a vendor paid on delivery. Each payout ties to a **SHIFT #** and a **CASHIER**, which is why it counts toward a drawer at reconciliation.

5. Click **Shifts**. **Expected:** **Shifts** loads with a simpler layout: a single **Total Shifts** count (6,308 on July 11) and no insight cards. The list **Shifts List** carries **+ New Record** and a search box. Columns: **SHIFT # · LOCATION · TERMINAL · OPENED · CLOSED · OPENING DRAWER · CLOSING DRAWER · STATUS**. A July 11 example row: **31012 · 301 - Middleburg Heights Bagley · CLE301-GRT · 2026-01-10 15:59 · — · $5.00 · — · Open** (an open shift shows a dash for CLOSED and CLOSING DRAWER until it closes). A shift is one drawer session: when a terminal opened and closed, the opening and closing cash counts, and its status. This is the page you open when a drawer will not balance. Compare **OPENING DRAWER** and **CLOSING DRAWER** against the shift's payments and payouts to find the gap.

6. Click **Declined Credit Card Log**. **Expected:** the page loads with the list titled **Declined Charges List**, a single KPI **Declined Charges (so far)** (26 on July 11), **+ New Record**, and a search box. Columns: **DATE · CUSTOMER · PLAN · AMOUNT · REASON · ATTEMPTS · LOCATION**. A July 11 example row: **2026-01-10 10:01 · VISA CARDHOLDER · Low Fat Unlimited · $0.00 · Declined · 10 · MOO08**. This logs declined recurring-plan authorizations. When a member says they paid but you see no payment on their account, start here: the **REASON** column tells you why the charge failed, and **ATTEMPTS** tells you how many times it retried (the July 11 rows show retry counts of 10).

7. Click **Gift Card Transactions**. **Expected:** the page loads. KPIs: **Total Transactions** (332,213 on July 11) and **Transactions (30d)** (10,305), with the two middle tiles naming the top transaction types for the window — on July 11 they read **SALE (30d)** 55% and **REDEEM (30d)** 45% (the generic **Top Type (30d)** / **2nd Type (30d)** labels from the July 9 error-state capture take the type names once data loads). Cards: **Gift Card Trend**, **By Type**, **Balance Health**. Search card **SEARCH GIFT CARD TRANSACTIONS** with **Filters ▾** and **Search**. The list **Gift Card Transaction List** (**332,213 RECORDS**) has **+ New Record**, **Export**, and columns **DATE · CARD NUMBER · TYPE · AMOUNT · BALANCE · CUSTOMER · LOCATION**. A July 11 example row: **2026-01-10 21:23 · 800139349533 · REDEEM · $16.00 · — · — · 508 - Norfolk Tidewater** (a SALE row shows a negative amount, e.g. $-11.00; on the July 11 rows the **BALANCE** and **CUSTOMER** cells rendered as dashes). This is every load and redeem against a gift card. Search a card number to trace its history. The **Balance Health** card notes it records completed activity only, not card status, so to issue or check a card go to CRM Gift Cards (Lesson 2.4).

8. Click **House Account Transactions**. **Expected:** the page loads with a single **Total HA Transactions** count. The list **House Account Transactions List** carries **+ New Record** and a search box. Columns: **DATE · TICKET # · ACCOUNT · CUSTOMER · LOCATION · TOTAL**. (This was the one page still in its **Loading…** state — KPI at a dash, no rows yet — in the July 11 capture; give it a moment or refresh. Row-level examples remain unobserved.) These are charges billed to a house account instead of paid at the register. Each row ties a ticket to the **ACCOUNT** it was charged to, which feeds that account's balance and invoicing in CRM House Accounts (Lesson 2.5).

9. Recap the seven pages against the questions they answer. **Expected:** you can now name the page for each common request. Line items on a sale go to **Ticket Details**. How a ticket was paid goes to **Payments**. Cash out of the drawer goes to **Payouts**. A drawer that will not balance goes to **Shifts**. A "member says they paid" dispute goes to **Declined Credit Card Log**. A gift-card trace goes to **Gift Card Transactions**. A charge billed to an account goes to **House Account Transactions**.

**Knowledge check.** Name the page you open for each of these: (1) a member disputes a declined card, (2) a drawer that will not balance at close, (3) petty cash paid out of a register, (4) tracing a charge against a gift card. Answers: (1) **Declined Credit Card Log**, (2) **Shifts**, (3) **Payouts**, (4) **Gift Card Transactions**.

## Expected results

You can open all seven Transactions pages and read each one's purpose from its KPIs and columns without hunting. You know which page holds line items, completed payments, register cash-outs, drawer sessions, declined authorizations, gift-card activity, and house-account charges. You also know the two shapes this menu uses: the fuller pages (**Ticket Details**, **Payments**, **Payouts**, **Gift Card Transactions**) carry a four-KPI strip, three insight cards, and a search-with-filters card, while the leaner pages (**Shifts**, **Declined Credit Card Log**, **House Account Transactions**) show a single count and a plain search box. Opening any page changes nothing.

## Notes and troubleshooting (observed behavior only)

- The July 11 credentialed re-capture caught six of the seven pages healthy — live rows and populated headline KPIs — which supplied the example rows and values now shown in steps 2–7. **House Account Transactions** was still in its **Loading…** state at capture, so its row-level examples remain unobserved. On July 9 every list had shown **Couldn't load records (Failed to fetch). Check your connection to the AI agent service.**; if you hit that message live, it points at the record-loading service, not at the page itself.
- **Drift (July 9 → July 11), three items.** (1) The sidebar gained a **Refund Approvals** entry between **Payments** and **Payouts** — nine pages now; the rendered narration (blocks s01 and s09) speaks eight/seven and is flagged for re-render. (2) **Ticket Details** grew from eight to twelve columns, adding **CATEGORY**, **PROFIT CENTER**, **SERVICE GROUP**, and **TAX**. (3) The two middle KPI tiles on **Payouts** and **Gift Card Transactions** turn out to be dynamic: with data loaded they label themselves with the top vendor/type names (Petty Cash / Misc.; SALE / REDEEM) rather than the generic Top/2nd labels seen in the July 9 error state.
- The 30-day tiles on **Ticket Details** and **Payments** (**Services/Discounts/Revenue (30d)**, **Top/2nd Method (30d)**, **Volume (30d)**) still showed dashes on July 11 even with rows loading, and the **By Method** card on Payments reads "Not available at this data volume." A dash is "no value loaded," not zero.
- Three of the seven "health" cards carry the same kind of scope note, and it is worth reading. **Payment Health** says approval and decline status are not tracked on Payments (completed transactions only). **Payout Status** says approval and dispute status are not tracked on Payouts (completed disbursements only). **Balance Health** says active and expired card status are not tracked on Gift Card Transactions (completed activity only). The takeaway: these pages are records of what completed, not workflow queues. Declines have their own page; card and account status live in CRM.
- Sidebar label versus page title: a few pages present a slightly different name once open. **Declined Credit Card Log** opens as **Declined Charges List**; **Payments** opens as **Sales Payments**. The sidebar label is the one to use when directing a colleague.
- **Filters**, **Search**, **Export**, and **+ New Record** appear on the fuller pages; **+ New Record** also appears on the three leaner pages. This lesson is a list-level tour, so none of those are opened here. **Export** is a download action and stays untouched under tenant rules.
- Menu structure differs from the legacy WashAssist Sales menu (no Adjustments, Credit Card Transactions, or Retail Customer Transactions page; Adjustments moved to Inventory). This is the project's existing mapping, DISCREPANCIES.md #10 — with one July 11 revision: a **Refund Approvals** sidebar entry now exists (see the drift note above), so the earlier "no Refunds Approval page" statement no longer holds. Its relationship to the Security Level refund-approval flags (Lesson 12.6) is unverified until the page is captured.
