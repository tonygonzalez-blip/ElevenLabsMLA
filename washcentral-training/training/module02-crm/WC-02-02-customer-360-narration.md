### Narration craft standard (project-wide)

1. **The 1-second visual buffer.** Never speak the instant a screen changes. Each action cue is followed by `<break time="1.4s" />` so the click and the tab swap land before the explanation begins.
2. **Varying sentence length.** Short fragments set against longer explanatory lines; no two adjacent blocks with the same rhythm.
3. **Show, don't read.** Narrate what a region means for the technician's ticket, not every label already on screen.

## Timed script

### Part 1 — Header band and tabs 1 to 5

[00:00 | Step 1 — Open Customer 360]
Click one row in the customer list. <break time="1.4s" /> That single click opens the whole member. Everything you need for most tickets is now on one page: who they are up top, their numbers just below, and the detail waiting in the tabs. Start at the top and work down.

[00:16 | Step 2 — Header identity]
Look at the header. <break time="1.4s" /> The avatar, the name, the plan badge, and the line beneath it tell you four things at a glance: who you have, what they pay for, where they wash, and whether the account is in good standing. Read that line first on every call. It confirms you are on the right person.

[00:33 | Step 3 — Status chips]
Now the chips under the name. <break time="1.2s" /> Plan active, a plate on file for the cameras, auto-pay running, texting turned on. Four little flags that answer half the "is my account set up right" questions before you open a single tab.

[00:47 | Step 4 — Header buttons]
Over on the right, four controls. <break time="1.4s" /> Back returns you to the list. Edit Customer opens the form we cover next lesson. Actions holds the member-level moves, and we open that at the end. The one you never touch is Delete.
_[Director: sweep the four buttons; do not click any. STOP ring on Delete.]_

[01:03 | Step 5 — Metric band]
Under the header sits a strip of six numbers. <break time="1.4s" /> Think of it as the account's vital signs: when they joined, what they spend, how often they come, their points, the next renewal, and the last time you saw them. When a member asks "how long have I been with you" or "when do I renew," the answer is right here, no digging.
_[Director: tile set confirmed July 11 (Member Since · Plan $/mo · Washes (30d) · Loyalty Points · Next Renewal · Last Visit); record the labels exactly as shown.]_

[01:27 | Step 6 — Related chips]
Below that, a row of shortcuts. <break time="1.2s" /> Each chip is a doorway into one slice of the account: their vehicles, their tickets, their wash books, gift cards, house account, loyalty, rewards. Instead of hunting, you click straight through to the part you need.

[01:44 | Step 7 — Overview tab]
The Overview tab opens first. <break time="1.4s" /> Up top, an A-I snapshot writes the account in plain language, with a Regenerate button to refresh it. Below are the charts: visits by month, the mix of services, spend over time, and points. Treat the snapshot as a helpful draft you confirm against the real tabs, the same rule you use for Shyne.
_[Director: do not click Regenerate.]_

[02:09 | Step 8 — Contact tab]
Switch to Contact. <break time="1.4s" /> This is the record itself: name and business details, address, phone and email, plus the notification and consent settings. You read it here; you change it on the edit form in the next lesson. For now it answers "is our contact info current."

[02:25 | Step 9 — Membership tab]
Open Membership. This is the one you will use most. <break time="1.4s" /> Plan details, the loyalty section with the bar code, the visit and spend totals, and the billing cycle. Two fields earn their keep here. The bar code is exactly what the POS scans, so this is your first stop when a tag will not read. And next charge tells the member when they get billed again.

[02:51 | Step 10 — Payments tab]
Now Payments. <break time="1.2s" /> The cards on file and any prepaid credit live here. You can see what is set up to bill, and you can see remaining book value. Reading it is safe. Adding a card is not part of this walk.
_[Director: do not click Add Card.]_

[03:07 | Step 11 — Vehicles tab]
Last tab in Part 1, Vehicles. <break time="1.4s" /> These are the plates the cameras match to the plan. You will also see a re-tracts list, the equipment holds set on this account's washes, like a skipped tire shine or top brush. When a member says the wash left something off, this is where you check.
_[Director: do not click Add Vehicle. End of Part 1; stay on the same record for Part 2.]_

### Part 2 — Tabs 6 to 9, Actions menu, and recap

[03:25 | Step 12 — Linked Accounts tab]
Linked Accounts. <break time="1.4s" /> Shared plans and household members show up here: a spouse on the same membership, a second driver, a family link. When one person is asking about another's plan, check this tab before you assume they are separate accounts.
_[Director: do not click Link Account.]_

[03:40 | Step 13 — Activity tab]
Activity is the running log. <break time="1.2s" /> Every visit and transaction, newest first, each with a type, a date, and an amount. When you need "what happened and when," you scroll this instead of piecing it together from tickets.

[03:56 | Step 14 — Messaging tab]
Open Messaging. <break time="1.4s" /> This is the two-way text thread with the member, and it shows whether they opted in. Read it to see what was already said, so you are not repeating a message someone sent yesterday. One hard rule: you never send from here in the demo. Read only.
_[Director: do not type, do not send. STOP ring on the compose box.]_

[04:18 | Step 15 — Notes tab]
Now Notes. <break time="1.4s" /> These are internal staff notes, and the label says it plainly: not visible to the customer. Below them, the full communication history by channel, and a change log. This is where you leave context for the next agent and where you find what a teammate already did. Save Note stays untouched today.
_[Director: do not click Save Note.]_

[04:41 | Step 16 — Actions menu]
Finally, open Actions. <break time="1.4s" /> Two items today: Issue Wash and Refund Customer. The old guide listed six, so if you are expecting Cancel or Pause here and they are missing, that gap is logged and parked, not something you broke. Read the list, then close it. Both of these move money or issue value, so in the demo you open the menu and stop.
_[Director: list the two items; do not click either. STOP rings on both. Close with Esc.]_

[05:07 | Step 17 — Recap]
That is the whole record. <break time="1.2s" /> Header and chips tell you who. The metric strip gives the headline numbers. The chips jump you into any slice. And nine tabs answer the everyday tickets: the bar code and the next charge on Membership, consent on Contact and Messaging, the internal trail on Notes. One page, read top to bottom, and you changed nothing to get every answer.
