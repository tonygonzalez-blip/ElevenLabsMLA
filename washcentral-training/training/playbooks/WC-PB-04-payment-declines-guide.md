# WC-PB-04 · Payment declines / auto-recharge failures (Troubleshooting Playbook)

v1.0 · July 9, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 9, 2026.**

## Purpose

A member says their card was declined, or a monthly auto-recharge never went through, or a site manager reports renewals failing across a lane. This playbook is the order you work those tickets: read the network baseline, decide how wide the problem is, then follow the trail to one customer, one location, or a messaging-charge question. You verify at every stop and change nothing. The fix actions (updating a card, editing gateway credentials) happen in production with the right role, never from the demo tenant.

Work it as a triage flow: the **Decline Rate** KPI tells you whether this is one card or a pattern, **Priority Intelligence** shows what WashCentral has already queued for recovery, and from there you branch to Customer 360, the **Declined Credit Card Log**, or a location's **Payments & Credentials** tab. Every screen in this lesson is read-only.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians on Tech Support and Onboarding who take billing and membership tickets. You should be signed in and comfortable with the global header and the left navigation rail (WC-01-01 and WC-01-02). No payment-processing background is assumed. This is a diagnosis-and-escalation playbook, so you read and route; you do not process, refund, or retry a charge here.

## Navigation path

Four modules carry this playbook, all reachable from the left rail: **Command Center** (the baseline and the recovery queue), **CRM → Customers → [customer row]** for a single member's Customer 360, **Sales → Declined Credit Card Log** for a batch of failures, **Settings → Locations → [location row] → Payments & Credentials** for a site-wide gateway problem, and **Billing → Usage & Billing** when the complaint is really about messaging charges.

## Steps

One action per step. Hold each new screen two to three seconds before moving on. Nothing in this lesson is created, edited, sent, or retried.

1. Open the **Command Center** and read the **Decline Rate** KPI in the left LIVE column. **Expected:** the tile reads **Decline Rate 0.24%**, subtitle "billing declines", with a trend chip of **−0.03%**. That is the network-wide baseline for card declines. A single member's failed charge does not move this number; a broad gateway or file problem does. Read it first so you know whether you are chasing one card or a pattern.
2. Scroll to the **Priority Intelligence** feed and find the billing-recovery items. Do not click any action row. **Expected:** a Revenue play reads "18 recoverable billing failures via card updater" with "Historical recovery rate ~62%" and a **+$640 Recover →** action, and an automation card reads "Payment retry automation — 37 queued, 11 recovered". These tell you WashCentral is already retrying failed charges through the card updater. **Recover →** launches an outreach flow, so leave it alone while diagnosing. **[READ-ONLY]**
3. Decide the scope of the ticket before you navigate anywhere. **Expected:** one of three branches. One customer who says "my card was declined" sends you to Customer 360 (steps 4 to 6). Several members at one site, or a manager reporting a wave of failures, sends you to the **Declined Credit Card Log** (steps 7 to 8) and, if it is site-wide, to the location's gateway settings (steps 9 to 10). A complaint about *messaging* charges rather than card declines sends you to Billing (step 11).
4. For the one-customer branch, open the member's Customer 360 from **CRM → Customers → [customer row]**. **Expected:** the nine-tab customer detail page opens (`crm-customer-detail.html`). This detail view needs a row-click parameter and was not captured in the July 9 corpus, so its live values are TBD — parked; the tab and field names below come from the July 8 verified walk (learning plan, Lesson 2.2) and are attributed as such.
5. Open the **Membership** tab and read the **BILLING CYCLE** block. **Expected:** *(source: learning plan, Lesson 2.2, July 8 verified walk)* the block lists **RENEWAL DATE**, **LAST CHARGE**, **NEXT CHARGE**, and **LAST PAYMENT RESULT**. For a declined-charge ticket, **LAST PAYMENT RESULT** tells you whether the most recent attempt failed and **NEXT CHARGE** tells you when the system will try again. The specific values for any given member are TBD — parked (the detail page is not in the July 9 corpus).
6. Open the **Payments** tab to check the card on file. **Expected:** *(source: learning plan, Lesson 2.2)* the tab shows **PAYMENT METHODS** (card on file and expiry) with **+ Add Card**, plus **PREPAID & CREDITS**. An expired or removed card is the most common single-customer cause. The fix is a card update: **Update Card** or a payment-update link sent to the member. That is a production action with the right role and is never sent from the demo tenant. Where the card values and the update control render for a live member is TBD — parked.
7. For the many-customers branch, go to **Sales → Declined Credit Card Log** and read the column headers. **Expected:** the page header reads **TRANSACTIONS · Declined Credit Card Log** with a KPI strip "— Declined Charges (so far)". The list columns are **DATE · CUSTOMER · PLAN · AMOUNT · REASON · ATTEMPTS · LOCATION**. This is the first stop when a member insists they paid but the record shows a decline: **REASON** and **ATTEMPTS** tell you why it failed and how many retries have run.
8. Use the search box to narrow the log, and read the load state. Do not click **+ New Record**. **Expected:** the search box placeholder reads "Search by customer or location…", and rows-per-page offers 25 / 50 / 100. In the July 9 capture the list body returned "Couldn't load records (Failed to fetch). Check your connection to the AI agent service.", so the column map is grounded but the actual rows are TBD — parked. When rows do load, filter by the member or the site the ticket names. **+ New Record** creates a record and is never clicked here. **[READ-ONLY]**
9. For a site-wide problem, go to **Settings → Locations**. **Expected:** the breadcrumb reads **Settings › Locations**; the **Locations▾** submenu lists **Locations**, **Location Groups**, **Location Sub-Brand**, **Payment Credential Approval**, and **Integrations**; the list columns are **CODE · NAME · ADDRESS · CITY · STATE · ZIP · PHONE · LAST SYNC**, above KPI tiles **6 Total Locations · 6 Active · 2 States · Last Sync Today**. (In the July 9 capture this list also returned "Couldn't load locations (Failed to fetch).", so the row values are TBD — parked; the columns and submenu are grounded.)
10. Open the affected location's detail and read the **Payments & Credentials** tab. Read only; change nothing. **Expected:** the location detail carries the tabs **Profile · Operations · Payments & Credentials · Messaging · Users**, and the credential section groups the processors into gateway blocks named **OPENEDGE / SMARTTECH / OTHER / CARDCONNECT** *(tab and block names per DISCREPANCIES.md #23; values are masked and never read aloud or written down)*. The plan's PB-4 also names **AUTO CHARGE DECLINE ATTEMPTS** and a **RECHARGE SCHEDULER** in this tab; the location detail page (`settings-locations-edit.html`) returned a 404 in the July 9 corpus, so those field contents are TBD — parked. Credential changes are made only through **Payment Credential Approval**, never hand-edited, so this tab is a read-and-record stop.
11. If the complaint is about messaging charges rather than card declines, go to **Billing → Usage & Billing** and read **Usage by Source**. **Expected:** the tabs read **Overview · SMS Usage · Email Usage · Invoices 1 open · Rate Card · Spending Alerts**, with **Export CSV**. The **Usage by Source — May 2026** panel, subtitle "Cost breakdown by campaign & automation", has columns **SOURCE · TYPE · VOLUME · COST · SHARE** and tags each row **Campaign**, **Automation**, or **Transactional**. This is where a "why was I charged" question about texts and emails gets answered, separate from card declines. Do not click **Export CSV**.
12. Package the escalation from what you read, and stop. **Expected:** a ticket that names the gateway (**OpenEdge / SmartTech / CardConnect / Other**), attaches a few decline samples with their **REASON** and **ATTEMPTS**, and gives counts and the affected **LOCATION** or member. You never paste card or credential values, even masked. Diagnosis and routing are your job on this playbook; the retry, the card update, and any credential change happen in production with the right role. **[READ-ONLY]**
13. Recap the path. **Expected:** you read the **Decline Rate** baseline, scoped the ticket to one customer or many, followed it to Customer 360, the **Declined Credit Card Log**, or a location's **Payments & Credentials**, checked Billing when the charge was really about messaging, and escalated with the gateway name, samples, and counts. Every screen was read-only.

## Expected results

You can tell a single failed card from a site-wide gateway problem by reading the **Decline Rate** KPI and **Priority Intelligence** before you navigate. You know where a member's **NEXT CHARGE** and **LAST PAYMENT RESULT** live (Customer 360 → **Membership**), where a batch of failures lists out (**Sales → Declined Credit Card Log**), where a site's gateway blocks sit (**Settings → Locations → [site] → Payments & Credentials**), and where messaging charges are broken out (**Billing → Usage & Billing → Usage by Source**). You escalate with a gateway name, decline samples, and counts, and you never move data or paste credentials.

**Knowledge check:** (1) Which one number tells you whether a decline is isolated or a pattern? (2) Where do you confirm a member's next charge date and last payment result? (3) Where do you pull a filtered list of failed charges for a site? (4) What three things go into the escalation, and what must never go into it?

## Notes and troubleshooting (observed behavior only)

- The **Decline Rate** KPI is a network baseline, not a per-customer figure. A single expired card will not shift 0.24%; a gateway outage or a bad credentials push will. Read the KPI to size the problem, then branch.
- Two pages in this playbook did not load their rows in the July 9 capture: the **Declined Credit Card Log** and the **Settings → Locations** list both returned "Couldn't load records/locations (Failed to fetch). Check your connection to the AI agent service." The columns, KPIs, and search boxes are grounded from the captured screen; the row contents are TBD — parked. Treat an empty log the same way on the live tenant: confirm the fetch actually returned data before telling a member there are no declines on file.
- The **Membership** billing fields and the **Payments** card-on-file view come from the July 8 verified walk (Lesson 2.2), because the Customer 360 detail page needs a row-click parameter and was not captured in the July 9 corpus. Author and record those field values against the live detail page at record time; the field labels are attributed, the values are parked.
- The location **Payments & Credentials** tab body is not in the July 9 corpus (`settings-locations-edit.html` returned a 404). The gateway block names **OPENEDGE / SMARTTECH / OTHER / CARDCONNECT** are grounded in DISCREPANCIES.md #23; the **AUTO CHARGE DECLINE ATTEMPTS** and **RECHARGE SCHEDULER** field contents are plan-sourced and TBD — parked.
- Acting on the membership itself (pausing or cancelling after repeated declines) is a separate flow. The Customer 360 **Actions ▾** menu showed only **Issue Wash** and **Refund Customer** in the walk; whether Cancel / Pause is role- or release-gated is TBD — parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #4).
- Every page here also shows the session idle behavior ("Still there?" with a 2:00 countdown) and the **Ask Shyne** header button. Both are out of scope for this playbook; session behavior is parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #9).
