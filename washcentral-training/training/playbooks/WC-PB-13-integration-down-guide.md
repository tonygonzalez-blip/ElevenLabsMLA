# WC-PB-13 · Integration down: Zoho, SSO, and accounting (Troubleshooting Playbook)

v1.0 · July 9, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 9, 2026.**

## Purpose

A site says help desk tickets stopped reaching Zoho, or a group of users who sign in with single sign-on can no longer get in, or the monthly accounting export came back with gaps. All three are the same class of ticket: a connector between WashCentral and an outside service has stopped authenticating. This playbook is the order you work it. You open the connector, read its status banner and its token, then check the surface the user actually feels, and you escalate with the integration name, its status, and the timestamps. Every screen here is read-only. The fix (re-authenticating, editing credentials) is an admin action in production, never something you run from the demo tenant.

You work the **Zoho Desk** connector as the example because it is the one captured in full today, but the read is the same for every connector: status chip, **Test Connection**, credential and token block. You never click **Test Connection**, and you never read a masked credential value into a ticket.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians on Tech Support and Onboarding who take integration and login tickets. You should be signed in and comfortable with the global header, the left rail, and the Settings navigation (WC-01-01 and WC-01-02). No integration-engineering background is assumed. This is a diagnosis-and-escalation playbook: you read the status and route it, you do not connect, re-authenticate, or edit any credential.

## Navigation path

Three areas carry this playbook, all reached from the left rail: **Settings → Integrations → [connector]** for the connector's own status and token (worked here as **Zoho Desk**), **IT & Support → Help Desk (Zoho)** for the Zoho ticket surface, and **Settings → Users & Security → System Users** for the **SSO** column when the symptom is a login failure. The accounting branch stays in **Settings → Integrations** on the accounting connector.

## Steps

One action per step. Hold each new screen two to three seconds before moving on. Nothing in this lesson is connected, edited, re-authenticated, or sent.

1. Scope the ticket to one connector before you open anything. **Expected:** the symptom points you at one of three branches. "Help desk tickets stopped reaching Zoho" is the **Zoho Desk** connector. "Users who sign in with single sign-on cannot get in" is the tenant's SSO provider under **Settings → Integrations**, and it shows up in **System Users**. "The accounting export is missing entries" is the accounting connector (**QuickBooks** or **Sage**, per the plan's PB-13 note) and, for mapping gaps, GL Codes. All three read the same way: open the connector, read its status, read its token, then confirm the surface.
2. Open the connector's Settings page. This playbook works **Settings → Integrations → Zoho Desk**. **Expected:** the breadcrumb reads **Settings › Zoho Desk**. The header shows the connector name **Zoho Desk**, a **Help Desk Integration** badge, and the status chip **⚠ Not Configured**, with the subtitle **Support Tickets · Settings** and the type chips **OAuth 2.0**, **Live View**, and **Server-side Token**. That status chip is the headline of the whole ticket: it tells you at a glance whether the connection is set up before you read anything else. In the demo tenant Zoho reads **⚠ Not Configured** because it was never connected; a tenant with a live connection shows a connected status instead. The exact connected-state label is not in this corpus, so treat it as TBD — parked.
3. Read the four summary tiles across the top of the connector card. **Expected:** the tiles read **Not set** (Connection), **US** (Data Center), **—** (Org ID), and **both** (Sync Direction). Connection **Not set** matches the **⚠ Not Configured** banner: nothing has authenticated. Data Center and Sync Direction tell you where the tenant points and which way records move. When a live connection drops, this row is the quick read before you go into the credentials.
4. Locate the two action buttons at the top-right, and do not click **Test Connection**. **Expected:** **Test Connection** and **Edit Configuration** sit at the top-right of the connector card. **Test Connection** fires a live probe against the real integration, so running it is an admin action on a production connector, not something you click while diagnosing from the demo tenant. **Edit Configuration** opens the credential form where re-authentication happens, also an admin action. On this playbook you read both and click neither. **[READ-ONLY]**
5. On the **Settings** tab, read the **CREDENTIALS** block and find **TOKEN EXPIRY**. **Expected:** the block lists **CLIENT ID** (—), **CLIENT SECRET** (**Not set**), **ORG ID** (—), **DATA CENTER** (us), **ACCESS TOKEN** (**Not set**), **REFRESH TOKEN** (**Not set**), **TOKEN EXPIRY** (—), and **ACCOUNTS SERVER** (—). For a connector that was working and then stopped, an empty or past **TOKEN EXPIRY** with a blank **ACCESS TOKEN** is the usual cause: the token lapsed and the connector can no longer authenticate. Re-authenticating happens through **Edit Configuration** with the right role, never from here. Masked or empty credential values are never read aloud or copied into a ticket.
6. Confirm the connector is switched on and read its sync setup, then note where the reconnect steps live. **Expected:** under **GENERAL**, **ENABLED** reads **Yes**; under **SYNC**, **SYNC DIRECTION** reads **both**. The connector is turned on and set to move records both ways, so the failure is authentication, not a toggle someone switched off. The **Instructions** tab, next to **Settings**, holds the step-by-step connect procedure; its body is not captured in this corpus, so treat it as the admin's reconnect reference, contents TBD — parked.
7. Symptom A, the Zoho help desk surface. Go to **IT & Support → Help Desk (Zoho)** to see whether tickets are landing. **Expected:** in the July 9 corpus this page (`zoho-tickets.html`) returned **HTTP Error 404 · Not Found**, so its empty-inbox state could not be observed and is TBD — parked. The **Help Desk (Zoho)** menu item itself is confirmed under IT & Support (DISCREPANCIES.md #17). Until that page loads, the connector's **⚠ Not Configured** status is your reliable signal that tickets are not syncing. (New discrepancy: the plan expects a Help Desk empty state here; the corpus shows the page returns a 404.)
8. Symptom B, single sign-on login failures. Go to **Settings → Users & Security → System Users** and read the column headers. **Expected:** the panel header reads **System Users List** with **— Total Users** above it, a search box placeholder **Search by name, code, or email…**, and the columns **USER NAME · USER CODE · USER LEVEL · EMAIL · PHONE · ACTIVE · SSO · LAST LOGIN**. The **SSO** column flags which accounts sign in through single sign-on; when the SSO connector is down, those are the users who cannot get in, and **LAST LOGIN** shows when each last succeeded. In the July 9 capture the list body returned "Couldn't load records (Failed to fetch). Check your connection to the AI agent service.", so the columns are grounded but the rows, including who is SSO-enabled, are TBD — parked. Do not click **+ New Record**. **[READ-ONLY]**
9. Symptom C, accounting export gaps. Stay in **Settings → Integrations** and open the accounting connector. **Expected:** the tenant's accounting connector (**QuickBooks** or **Sage**, per the plan's PB-13 note) carries the same layout as Zoho: a status chip, **Test Connection**, and a credential and token block. Its specific page was not captured in this playbook's scope, so its field contents are TBD — parked; read its status chip and token the same way you read Zoho's. Gaps that trace to code mapping rather than the connection are chased through GL Codes mapping (Lesson 12.4), not here.
10. Rule out a transient data-service error before you blame the connector. **Expected:** two screens in this playbook returned "Couldn't load records (Failed to fetch). Check your connection to the AI agent service." in the July 9 capture, and **Help Desk (Zoho)** returned a 404. An empty list or a failed page is not proof the integration is down; it can be the app's own data service failing to load. Reload, and confirm the connector's own status chip and token state before you tell anyone the integration is broken.
11. Package the escalation and stop. **Expected:** a ticket that names the integration (**Zoho Desk**, the SSO provider, or the accounting connector), quotes its status from the banner (for Zoho today, **⚠ Not Configured**), states whether **Test Connection** was run and what it returned (run by an admin, not from the demo tenant), and gives the timestamps of the failure window. You never paste a token, a client secret, or any masked credential, even in part. Re-authentication and credential changes are admin actions through **Edit Configuration**; on this playbook you read the status and route it. **[READ-ONLY]**
12. Recap the path. **Expected:** you scoped the symptom to one connector, read its status banner and **TOKEN EXPIRY**, located **Test Connection** without running it, then checked the surface the user feels: the **Help Desk (Zoho)** page, the **SSO** column in **System Users**, or the accounting connector. You ruled out a transient fetch error, and you escalated with the integration name, its status, and the timestamps. Every screen was read-only.

## Expected results

You can turn "the integration is down" into a specific, routable ticket. You know the status banner (**⚠ Not Configured** for Zoho today) is the first read, that **TOKEN EXPIRY** and a blank **ACCESS TOKEN** point at a lapsed login, and that **Test Connection** is an admin probe you locate but never click. You know where each symptom surfaces: the **Help Desk (Zoho)** page for Zoho tickets, the **SSO** column in **System Users** for single-sign-on lockouts, and the accounting connector plus GL Codes for export gaps. You rule out a transient fetch error, and you escalate with the integration name, the status, and the timestamps, without moving data or pasting a credential.

**Knowledge check:** (1) On a connector page, which single element do you read first, and what does it tell you? (2) Which credential field points at a lapsed login, and whose job is the re-auth? (3) Which column in System Users tells you a user signs in through single sign-on? (4) Before you tell a site the integration is down, what has to be ruled out, and why? (5) What goes into the escalation, and what must never go into it?

## Notes and troubleshooting (observed behavior only)

- The **Zoho Desk** connector reads **⚠ Not Configured** in the demo tenant, with Connection **Not set** and every credential blank or **Not set**. That is a never-connected state, not a mid-session failure. The connected-state banner and the "Connection" tile value for a live tenant are not in this corpus and are TBD — parked; author and record them against a connected connector when one is available.
- **Test Connection** is on the forbidden list for a reason: it is a live probe against the real integration. Locate it, point to it in the recording, and never click it. If an admin runs it, quote the result in the escalation rather than running it yourself.
- The **Help Desk (Zoho)** page (`zoho-tickets.html`) returned **HTTP Error 404** in the July 9 capture, so the "empty inbox" state the plan describes could not be observed. The menu item is confirmed under IT & Support (DISCREPANCIES.md #17); the page's contents are TBD — parked. This sits next to the known 404 family (LogicPOS, PARKED-ITEMS.md #2): a 404 does not prove a feature is gone, so route it, do not tell the site it does not exist.
- **System Users** listed its **SSO** and **LAST LOGIN** columns but returned "Couldn't load records (Failed to fetch). Check your connection to the AI agent service." for its rows. The column map is grounded; the rows, and therefore which users are SSO-enabled, are TBD — parked. Confirm the fetch actually returned data before telling anyone no SSO users exist.
- The same "Failed to fetch" data-service error appears on more than one list page in this corpus, so treat an empty list as unconfirmed, not as evidence. Reload and read the connector's own status chip before concluding an integration is down.
- The accounting branch (**QuickBooks** / **Sage**) and the tenant's SSO provider pages were not captured in this playbook's scope. Their layout mirrors Zoho (status chip, **Test Connection**, token block), but their specific field labels and values are TBD — parked; ground them against those pages when authoring the accounting and SSO connector lessons.
- Every page here also shows the session idle behavior ("Still there?" with a 2:00 countdown and the **Stay Logged In** / **Stay Active** controls) and the **Ask Shyne** header button. Both are out of scope for this playbook; session behavior is parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #9).
