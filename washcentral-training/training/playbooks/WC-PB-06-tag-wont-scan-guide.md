# WC-PB-06 · My tag/plate won't scan (Troubleshooting Playbook)

v1.0 · July 9, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 9, 2026.**

## Purpose

A member pulls up to the gate and the camera reads their plate, or the greeter scans their tag, and nothing happens: the wash is not granted, or the POS says the member is not recognized. This playbook is the order you work that ticket. You start on the member's own account, widen to the plate record, check whether the plate is blocked, and only then look at the site hardware. Every screen here is read-only. You diagnose and route; you never edit a vehicle, blacklist a plate, or change a membership from the demo tenant.

The logic is a funnel. Most "won't scan" tickets are one member with a lapsed plan, a past-due charge, or a plate that was never linked, and those answers sit in Customer 360. If the account is clean, the plate record in the master list tells you whether the plate is on the right customer and active. If that is clean too, the **Blacklisted** list and the site's hardware alerts are where a gate-level block or a camera fault shows up. Work it top to bottom and you rarely reach the bottom.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians on Tech Support and Onboarding who take gate, membership, and ALPR tickets. You should be signed in and comfortable with the global header and the left navigation rail (WC-01-01 and WC-01-02) and with Customer 360 (Lesson 2.2). No ALPR or hardware background is assumed. This is a diagnosis-and-escalation playbook: you read the account, the plate, and the alerts, then hand a clean ticket upstream.

## Navigation path

Four areas carry this playbook, all reachable from the left rail. **CRM → Customers → [customer row] → Customer 360** (`crm-customer-detail.html`) holds the member's barcode, plan status, and linked plate. **CRM → Vehicles** is the master vehicle list you search by plate. **Settings → Users & Security → Blacklisted** is the plate block list. **IT & Support → System Alerts** and the **IT & Support** Support Queue are the site-hardware trail. One reason-code check lives in **Settings → Operations → Cancellation Reasons**.

## Steps

One action per step. The same step numbers run through the shot list and the narration. Hold each new screen two to three seconds before moving on. Nothing here is created, edited, linked, or blocked.

1. Open **CRM → Vehicles** and read the KPI band, then frame the ticket before you touch the member's record. **Expected:** the top band reads **3,892 Total Vehicles**, **2,104 Active Members**, **14 Flagged**, and **87 New This Month**, and the **Vehicle Status** panel shows **94% ACTIVE**, **5% INACTIVE**, and **1% FLAGGED** ("3,659 active · 233 inactive / flagged"). Read **Flagged** first: fourteen flagged plates network-wide means this is almost certainly one member, not a wave. Then work the funnel in order: the member's account, the plate record, the blocklist, the site hardware. You come back to this list to search the exact plate in step 4.
2. Open the member's Customer 360 from **CRM → Customers → [customer row]**, go to the **Membership** tab, and read the **LOYALTY & POINTS** and **PLAN DETAILS** groups. **Expected:** *(source: learning plan, Lesson 2.2, July 8 verified walk)* **LOYALTY & POINTS** lists **LOYALTY CARD**, **BARCODE**, **TEMP BARCODE**, **ACCUMULATES POINTS**, and **REWARDS EXPIRE**; **PLAN DETAILS** lists **MEMBERSHIP TYPE**, **HOME LOCATION**, **MEMBER SINCE**, **STATUS**, **MONTHS REMAINING**, and **REWARD PROGRAM**; **BILLING CYCLE** lists **RENEWAL DATE**, **LAST CHARGE**, **NEXT CHARGE**, and **LAST PAYMENT RESULT**. This is the first stop for a "won't scan" ticket: the **BARCODE** is what the POS reads, **STATUS** tells you the plan is **Active**, and **NEXT CHARGE** tells you it is not past due. The Customer 360 view page needs a row-click parameter and was not captured in the July 9 corpus, so the field labels here are attributed to the July 8 walk and the live values for any given member are TBD — parked.
3. On the same record, read the **Plate linked** header chip, then open the **Vehicles** tab. **Expected:** *(source: learning plan, Lesson 2.2)* the header status chips include **★ Unlimited Plus**, **Plate linked**, **Auto-pay active**, and **SMS subscribed**, and the Related chips row includes a **Vehicles** count chip. The **Vehicles** tab shows **VEHICLES ON ACCOUNT** (with a **+ Add Vehicle** control) and a **RETRACTS** list of the equipment holds on this account's washes. **Plate linked** confirms a plate is on file for the cameras; if that chip is absent or the plate on file is wrong, the gate has nothing to match. Do not add a vehicle. The chip states and the plates on file are TBD — parked (the view page is not in the July 9 corpus).
4. Go back to **CRM → Vehicles** and search the master list for the member's exact plate. **Expected:** the search box placeholder reads "Search by plate, customer, make, or location…", and the list columns are **LICENSE PLATE · MAKE / MODEL · YEAR · CUSTOMER · LOCATION · STATUS · LAST VISIT**, with **Filters** and a **Search** button beside the box. Confirm three things when a row loads: the plate is attached to the **CUSTOMER** you expect, the **STATUS** is sane (not inactive or flagged), and **LAST VISIT** matches what the member describes. In the July 9 capture the list body returned "Couldn't load vehicles (Failed to fetch). Check your connection to the AI agent service.", so the column map is grounded but the actual rows are TBD — parked. Do not click **+ Add Vehicle**. **[READ-ONLY]**
5. Go to **Settings → Users & Security → Blacklisted** and check whether the plate is blocked. **Expected:** the breadcrumb reads **Settings › Blacklisted**, the KPI reads **— Total Blacklisted**, the search box placeholder reads "Search blacklisted plates…", and the list columns are **PLATE · TERMINAL · CREATED BY · CREATED**. A plate on this list is refused at the gate no matter how healthy the membership looks, so a clean account plus a "won't scan" symptom points you here. In the July 9 capture the list body returned "Couldn't load blacklisted plates (Failed to fetch).", showing "0–0 of 0", so whether a specific plate is listed is TBD — parked. Do not click **+ New Record**: adding or removing a blacklisted plate is a forbidden change from the demo tenant. **[READ-ONLY]**
6. If the account and the plate are both clean, take the site-hardware angle: open **IT & Support → System Alerts**. **Expected:** **System Alerts** is a listed item in the **IT & Support** left navigation (alongside **ML Command Center**, **Site Detail (Demo)**, **Support Tickets**, **Help Desk (Zoho)**, **Knowledge Base**, **Training Requests**, and the **Audit** group). This is where a tunnel or POS fault at the affected location would surface. In the July 9 corpus the page itself (`support-alerts.html`) returned **HTTP 404**, so the alert types the plan describes (Tunnel and POS) and any live alerts are TBD — parked. Open this page live at record time; if it still 404s, hold the parked card and route the hardware question to the site team.
7. Still in **IT & Support**, open the Support Queue and look for an open ticket at the affected site. **Expected:** the queue header reads **Support Queue**, the **Support Ticket List** ("OPEN AND RESOLVED TICKETS") columns are **TICKET · CATEGORY · LOCATION · OWNER · AGE · PRIORITY**, and a **SLA WATCH** panel flags breaching tickets. In the July 9 capture the queue held an open **LPR** ticket (**SUP-1042 · LPR · Miami North · High**) and a **Kiosk** ticket (**SUP-1036 · Kiosk · Orlando Central · Medium**). An existing **LPR** (license-plate recognition) or **Kiosk** ticket at the member's home location tells you the gate hardware is already under investigation, which changes your ticket from "one member" to "attach to the known site issue." Do not click **Create ticket** here unless the flow calls for a new one. **[READ-ONLY]**
8. Note the known field-failure reason so the ticket carries it. **Expected:** in **Settings → Operations → Cancellation Reasons** the list columns are **DESCRIPTION · SHOW ON POS**. The learning plan (PB-6, from the July 8 walk) records that the migrated reason set includes **RFID Tag Issues / Not Reading / cross read activate**, a recognized tag-read failure mode worth naming in the escalation. In the July 9 capture this list also returned "Couldn't load cancellation reasons (Failed to fetch).", so the specific reason rows are plan-sourced and TBD — parked; the column layout is grounded. **[READ-ONLY]**
9. Package the escalation from what you read, and stop. **Expected:** a ticket that states which checks passed and which failed: barcode present and plan **Active** (or not), plate **linked** (or not), the master-list **STATUS** for the plate, whether the plate appears on **Blacklisted**, and any open **LPR** or **Kiosk** ticket or **System Alert** at the site, tagged with **RFID Tag Issues / Not Reading** when the symptom fits. Name the location. You never blacklist, unblock, relink, or edit a plate from the demo: the fix (a plate relink, a blocklist removal, a hardware dispatch) happens in production with the right role. **[READ-ONLY]**
10. Recap the path. **Expected:** you framed the ticket on **CRM → Vehicles** (one plate, not a wave), read the member's **BARCODE**, **STATUS**, and **NEXT CHARGE** on Customer 360 **Membership**, confirmed the **Plate linked** chip and the **Vehicles** tab, searched the master vehicle list for the plate, checked **Blacklisted**, and worked the hardware angle through **System Alerts** and the Support Queue's **LPR** ticket, noting the **RFID Tag Issues** reason. Every screen was read-only.

## Expected results

You can take a "my tag won't scan" ticket and resolve or route it without changing a thing. You know the member's own account comes first: the **BARCODE**, **STATUS**, and **NEXT CHARGE** on Customer 360 **Membership**, and the **Plate linked** chip and **Vehicles** tab on the same record. You know the plate record lives in **CRM → Vehicles** (columns **LICENSE PLATE · MAKE / MODEL · YEAR · CUSTOMER · LOCATION · STATUS · LAST VISIT**), the block list lives in **Settings → Users & Security → Blacklisted** (**PLATE · TERMINAL · CREATED BY · CREATED**), and the hardware trail lives in **IT & Support → System Alerts** and the Support Queue, where an open **LPR** ticket reframes a single complaint as a known site issue. You escalate with the checks you ran, the location, and the **RFID Tag Issues** reason when it applies, and you never edit or block a plate from the demo.

**Knowledge check:** (1) Which one field on Customer 360 is the barcode the POS actually scans, and which tab holds it? (2) A member's account is healthy and the plate is on the right customer, yet the gate refuses them. Which single list do you check next? (3) Where do you find out whether the site's plate-reading hardware is already under a ticket? (4) What migrated cancellation reason should you name when a tag reads intermittently, and what are you never allowed to do to a plate from the demo tenant?

## Notes and troubleshooting (observed behavior only)

- The **Membership** barcode and plan fields and the **Plate linked** chip come from the July 8 verified walk (Lesson 2.2), because the Customer 360 view page (`crm-customer-detail.html`) needs a row-click parameter and was not captured in the July 9 static sweep. Author and record those values against the live detail page at record time; the field and chip labels are attributed, the values are TBD — parked.
- Three list pages in this playbook returned "Couldn't load … (Failed to fetch). Check your connection to the AI agent service." in the July 9 capture: **CRM → Vehicles**, **Settings → Blacklisted**, and **Settings → Cancellation Reasons**. Their columns, KPIs, and search boxes are grounded from the captured screen; the row contents are TBD — parked. Treat an empty list the same way on the live tenant: confirm the fetch actually returned data before telling a member their plate is not blacklisted or not on file.
- The **System Alerts** page (`support-alerts.html`) returned **HTTP 404** in the July 9 corpus, so the alert types (the plan names Tunnel and POS) could not be observed and are TBD — parked. The **System Alerts** navigation item itself is grounded in the **IT & Support** sidebar. Walk this page live before recording; if it 404s on the live tenant, route the hardware angle to the site team and note it.
- The migrated cancellation reason **RFID Tag Issues / Not Reading / cross read activate** is plan-sourced (PB-6, July 8 walk) and did not re-load in the July 9 **Cancellation Reasons** capture, so the exact reason strings are TBD — parked. Name the failure mode in the ticket; confirm the exact wording against the live reason list.
- The Support Queue distinguishes an **LPR** category from a **Kiosk** and a **Payments** category. For a plate/tag symptom, the **LPR** ticket is the one to match against; **Kiosk** points at the greeter terminal instead. Both were present in the July 9 capture (**SUP-1042 · LPR · Miami North**, **SUP-1036 · Kiosk · Orlando Central**).
- Membership-lifecycle actions (pause, cancel, reset) are out of scope here. The Customer 360 **Actions ▾** menu showed only **Issue Wash** and **Refund Customer** in the walk; whether the others are role- or release-gated is TBD — parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #4).
- Every page here also shows the session idle behavior ("Still there?" with a 2:00 countdown) and the **Ask Shyne** header button. Both are out of scope for this playbook; session behavior is parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #9).
