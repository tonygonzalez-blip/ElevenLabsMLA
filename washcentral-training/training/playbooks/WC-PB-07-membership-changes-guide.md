# WC-PB-07 · Membership change requests (Troubleshooting Playbook)

v1.0 · July 9, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 9, 2026.**

## Purpose

A member asks to change their membership: cancel it, pause it, reset the billing date, or get a refund. This playbook is the order you work those requests. You confirm what the account holds, read which actions the platform actually offers, check who is allowed to approve a refund, pick the standard reason, and log the whole interaction where it belongs. You verify at every stop and change nothing. The change itself, whether a refund or a cancel, happens in production with the right role, never from the demo tenant.

The important early check is what the green **Actions ▾** menu on Customer 360 gives you. In the live demo it offers two actions, **Issue Wash** and **Refund Customer**, not the six the legacy guide describes. Knowing that up front keeps you from promising a member a cancel or pause you cannot perform, and tells you when a request has to route to someone with the right role.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians on Tech Support and Onboarding who take membership and billing tickets. You should be signed in and comfortable with the global header and the left navigation rail (WC-01-01 and WC-01-02), and you should have worked Customer 360 once (Lesson 2.2). No refund-processing background is assumed. This is a diagnosis-and-routing playbook: you read the account, check the permission gate, and log the request; you do not cancel, pause, reset, or refund from the demo.

## Navigation path

Three surfaces carry this playbook. **CRM → Customers → [customer row]** opens the member's Customer 360 (`crm-customer-detail.html`), where the **Actions ▾** menu and the **Notes** tab live. **Settings → Users & Security → Security Level** is the refund-approval gate. **Settings → Operations → Refund Reasons** is the standard reason list. Every screen here is read-only.

## Steps

One action per step. Hold each new screen two to three seconds before moving on. Nothing in this lesson is cancelled, paused, reset, refunded, saved, or sent.

1. Open the member's Customer 360 from **CRM → Customers → [customer row]**. **Expected:** the nine-tab customer detail page opens (`crm-customer-detail.html`). The header band carries **← Back**, **Delete**, **Edit Customer**, and the green **Actions ▾**; the tabs are **Overview, Contact, Membership, Payments, Vehicles, Linked Accounts, Activity, Messaging, Notes**. *(Source: learning plan, Lesson 2.2, July 8 verified walk; this page is not in the July 9 corpus, so the live values are TBD — parked.)* **[READ-ONLY]**
2. Open the **Membership** tab and confirm what the member actually holds before you change anything. **Expected:** *(source: learning plan, Lesson 2.2)* the **PLAN DETAILS** block shows **MEMBERSHIP TYPE**, **HOME LOCATION**, **STATUS**, and **MONTHS REMAINING**, and the **BILLING CYCLE** block shows **RENEWAL DATE**, **LAST CHARGE**, and **NEXT CHARGE**. Read **STATUS** and **NEXT CHARGE** first: a cancel or pause request only makes sense against an active plan, and the next-charge date tells you how urgent the timing is. The specific values for a given member are TBD — parked (the detail page is not in the July 9 corpus).
3. Open the green **Actions ▾** menu to list the actions the platform offers. Do not click any item. **Expected:** the menu lists exactly two, **Issue Wash** and **Refund Customer**. The legacy six-action set (which included **Cancel Membership**, **Reset Membership**, and **Pause Membership**) does not appear live; whether those are role-gated, release-gated, or removed is TBD — parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #4). List the items, then close the menu without selecting either. *(Source: DISCREPANCIES.md #6 and the July 8 walk; not in the July 9 corpus.)* **[READ-ONLY]**
4. Match the request to what the menu gives you. **Expected:** two branches. A **refund** is a live action, so you gate it through the approval check and the reason list (steps 5 to 7). A **cancel, pause, or reset** is not a demo action, so you confirm your role can perform it on production and route it if it cannot (step 8). Either way, the interaction gets logged (step 9). Decide the branch here before you navigate.
5. For a refund, check whether the requesting user is even allowed to approve one: **Settings → Users & Security → Security Level**. **Expected:** the **Security Levels** page opens, breadcrumb **Settings › Security Levels**. The list columns are **NAME**, **LEVEL**, and **REFUND APPROVAL REQUIRED**; the KPI band across the top reads **Total Levels**, **Require Refund Approval**, and **No Approval Needed** (each shows a dash in place of a count); the search box placeholder reads "Search security levels…". In the July 9 capture the list body returned "Couldn't load security levels (Failed to fetch). Check your connection to the AI agent service.", so the per-level rows are TBD — parked; the columns, KPIs, and search box are grounded. **[READ-ONLY]**
6. Read the **REFUND APPROVAL REQUIRED** column against the requester's security level. **Expected:** this column marks, per level, whether a refund needs sign-off before it can be processed. Per the July 8 verified walk (Lesson 12.6; PARKED-ITEMS.md #5), the **Low**, **LowMedium**, and **Medium** levels require approval, so a Low or Medium user hands the refund to a higher-level approver (**HighMedium** or **High**) rather than processing it. The live Yes/No values were not observable on July 9 (Failed to fetch), so the exact per-level mapping is TBD — parked and confirmed against the live page at ticket time. *(Attributed; not in the July 9 corpus as rendered values.)*
7. Pick the standard refund reason from **Settings → Operations → Refund Reasons**. **Expected:** the **Refund Reasons** page opens, breadcrumb **Settings › Refund Reasons**, with a single **DESCRIPTION** column, a **Total Reasons** KPI (showing a dash in place of a count), and the search placeholder "Search refund reasons…". Every refund is tagged with one of these standard reasons for reporting. In the July 9 capture the list returned "Couldn't load refund reasons (Failed to fetch). Check your connection to the AI agent service.", so the actual reason entries are TBD — parked; the page structure is grounded. **[READ-ONLY]**
8. For a cancel, pause, or reset, confirm your role and route it. **Expected:** because those actions are not in the demo **Actions ▾** menu (step 3), you do not promise the member a change you cannot see a control for. On production you confirm your role can perform the action before acting; if it cannot, you route the request to a user who can. This is the open gap, TBD — parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #4): the demo does not expose Cancel/Reset/Pause, so the exact control and its permission rule are confirmed live before you commit to a member. Change nothing here. **[READ-ONLY]**
9. Log the interaction in Customer 360 → **Notes**. **Expected:** *(source: learning plan, Lesson 2.2)* the **Notes** tab holds Customer Notes labeled "Internal staff notes — not visible to customer" with **Pin to top**, **Clear**, and **Save Note** controls, and below it a **Communication History** panel with channel filters **All / SMS / Email / Calls / Push / In-App** and a **Change History** panel. Record what the member asked for, what you did or routed, and any approval, and pin it if the account is critical. Never log the change in the **Messaging** tab: that tab texts the customer. The field labels are attributed and the live values are TBD — parked (the detail page is not in the July 9 corpus). **[READ-ONLY]**
10. Package the escalation from what you read, and stop. **Expected:** a note or ticket that names the member, the exact change requested (cancel / pause / reset / refund), the refund reason if one applies, the requester's security level and whether approval is required, and who the request routes to. You verified and logged; you did not cancel, pause, reset, or refund from the demo tenant. **[READ-ONLY]**
11. Recap the path. **Expected:** you confirmed the plan on the **Membership** tab, listed the live **Actions ▾** (**Issue Wash**, **Refund Customer**) and knew Cancel/Pause/Reset are parked, checked the refund-approval gate in **Security Levels**, pulled the standard reason from **Refund Reasons**, and logged the request in **Notes**, never in Messaging. Every screen was read-only.

## Expected results

You can size a membership-change request before you touch anything: read the plan **STATUS** and **NEXT CHARGE** on the **Membership** tab, then read the **Actions ▾** menu to see what the platform will actually let you do. You know a refund is gated by the **REFUND APPROVAL REQUIRED** column on **Security Levels** and tagged from the **Refund Reasons** list, and that a cancel, pause, or reset is not a demo action and routes by role (PARKED-ITEMS.md #4). You log the whole interaction in Customer 360 → **Notes**, pin it when the account is critical, and you never use **Messaging** to record it because that texts the member. You escalate with the member, the requested change, the reason, and the approval status, and you change nothing.

**Knowledge check:** (1) Which two actions does the Customer 360 **Actions ▾** menu offer in the demo, and which membership actions are parked? (2) Which page and column tell you whether a user can approve a refund without sign-off? (3) Where do the standard refund reasons come from? (4) Where do you log a membership-change request, and which tab must you never use for it, and why?

## Notes and troubleshooting (observed behavior only)

- The Customer 360 **Actions ▾** menu, the **Membership** tab, and the **Notes** tab all live on `crm-customer-detail.html`, which needs a row-click parameter and was not captured in the July 9 corpus. Their labels are attributed to the July 8 verified walk (Lesson 2.2) and to DISCREPANCIES.md #6; author and record the live values against the real detail page at record time, and hold the parked card wherever a value cannot be shown.
- The membership lifecycle actions Cancel / Reset / Pause are the core open gap for this playbook. The demo **Actions ▾** menu showed only **Issue Wash** and **Refund Customer** on July 8; the legacy guide lists six actions. Whether Cancel/Reset/Pause are role-gated, release-gated, or removed is TBD — parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #4). Do not tell a member a cancel or pause "cannot be done": confirm the control and the role on production first.
- Both settings pages in this playbook failed to load their rows in the July 9 capture. **Security Levels** returned "Couldn't load security levels (Failed to fetch). Check your connection to the AI agent service.", and **Refund Reasons** returned "Couldn't load refund reasons (Failed to fetch). …". The columns, KPIs, and search boxes are grounded from the captured screens; the row values (the security levels and their approval flags, and the refund reason list) are TBD — parked. Treat an empty list the same way on the live tenant: confirm the fetch returned data before telling anyone a level or a reason does not exist.
- The per-level refund mapping (Low / LowMedium / Medium require approval; route to HighMedium or High) comes from the July 8 verified walk (Lesson 12.6; PARKED-ITEMS.md #5), because the July 9 list did not render its rows. Confirm the exact Yes/No per level against the live **REFUND APPROVAL REQUIRED** column when you work the ticket.
- Logging a membership change belongs in **Notes**, never **Messaging**. The **Messaging** tab is a two-way SMS thread with the customer, so anything typed there can be sent to the member. Internal record-keeping goes in Customer Notes, pinned if account-critical.
- Every page here also shows the session idle behavior ("Still there?" with a 2:00 countdown) and the **Ask Shyne** header button. Both are out of scope for this playbook; session behavior is parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #9).
