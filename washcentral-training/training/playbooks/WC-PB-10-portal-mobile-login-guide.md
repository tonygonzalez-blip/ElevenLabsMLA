# WC-PB-10 · Portal/mobile "can't log in / can't see feature" (Troubleshooting Playbook)

v1.0 · July 9, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 9, 2026.**

## Purpose

A member says the online portal won't take their login, or the mobile app rejects them, or they are signed in but a feature they expect is missing. This playbook is the order you work those tickets: confirm the portal is up and on the right address, then narrow to the account, the login rules, or a switched-off feature. You read at every stop and change nothing. The fixes (turning a login method on, flipping a module, publishing the portal) happen in production with the right role, never from the demo tenant.

Work it as a triage flow. The **● Live** chip and the **Logins Today** KPI tell you whether this is one member or the whole portal. The **Access & Security** card and its **Authentication** tab explain a rejected login. **Features & Modules** and the mobile **Mobile App Users** row explain a missing feature. Every screen in this playbook is read-only.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians on Tech Support and Onboarding who take portal and mobile-app tickets. You should be signed in and comfortable with the global header, the left navigation rail, and the Settings area (WC-01-01, WC-01-02, and Lesson 12.8). No portal-administration background is assumed. This is a diagnosis-and-escalation playbook, so you read and route; you do not publish the portal, save a setting, or edit an account here.

## Navigation path

Two modules carry this playbook. The portal side lives under **Settings → Customer Portal**: the hub at **Portal Overview**, the login rules at **Access & Security**. The mobile side lives on the main rail at **Digital → Mobile App Users**. Both are reachable from any page.

## Steps

One action per step. Hold each new screen two to three seconds before moving on. Nothing in this playbook is created, edited, saved, published, or sent.

1. Open the **Customer Portal** hub and read the portal identity and status. Navigate **Settings → Customer Portal → Portal Overview**. **Expected:** the header names the portal **Main Kiosk Flow Portal** with a **● Live** status chip; the line beneath reads "Settings · Customer Portal · portalv2.wash.me/MLASALE" with a **Copy** button and two chips, **Main Kiosk Portal** and **v2.4.1**. Top-right sit **← Settings**, **Preview Portal**, and **Publish Changes**. The **● Live** chip is your first check: if the portal is not Live, no member can reach the sign-in and the ticket is portal-wide, not one account. Read only; never click **Publish Changes**. **[READ-ONLY]**
2. Read the portal KPI strip across the top. **Expected:** five tiles read **1,840 Registered Members**, **247 Logins Today**, **4m 32s Avg Session**, **98.4% Uptime (30 days)**, and **68% Mobile Users**. **247 Logins Today** tells you other members are getting in right now, which points you at a single account rather than the whole portal. Zero logins on a Live portal is the opposite signal: something is blocking everyone.
3. Confirm the sign-in address and reproduce the failure. Click **Copy** to grab the exact portal URL, **portalv2.wash.me/MLASALE**, and compare it to the link the member is using; a stale or wrong URL is the most common "I can't log in". Then click **Preview Portal** to open the member-facing sign-in read-only and try to reproduce what they see. The **Portal Health** panel on this page backs you up: **API ENDPOINT Connected** and **SSL CERTIFICATE Valid** mean the portal backend is up, so a login failure is about the account or a setting, not the server. Do not click **Publish Changes**. **[READ-ONLY]**
4. Quick-triage the two hub cards that settle most of these tickets: **Access & Security** and **Features & Modules**. **Expected:** the **Access & Security** card (subtitle "Login methods, OTP, session rules") shows **LOGIN METHOD Phone & Email**, **OTP REQUIRED On**, and **SESSION TIMEOUT 30 min**. The **Features & Modules** card (subtitle "Toggle portal capabilities on/off") shows **ENABLED MODULES 7 of 9**, with **WASH HISTORY On** and **FAMILY PLAN On**. For a "can't log in" ticket, read the Access & Security card. For a "can't see a feature" ticket, that **7 of 9** line is the answer more often than not: two modules are simply switched off.
5. Open **Access & Security** and read the **LOGIN METHODS** block on the **Authentication** tab. Navigate **Settings → Customer Portal → Access & Security**. **Expected:** the header reads **Access & Security** with subtitle "Authentication, sessions & allowed domains", chips **Main Kiosk Portal / Live / v2.4.1**, and a status strip **Portal Status Live · Last Published Apr 22, 2026 · Version v2.4.1**. Three tabs sit below, **Authentication**, **Session & Limits**, and **Allowed Domains**, open on **Authentication**. Under **LOGIN METHODS**: **LOGIN BY PHONE Yes**, **LOGIN BY EMAIL Yes**, **LOGIN BY MEMBERSHIP NUMBER No**, **GUEST ACCESS No**. A member trying to sign in with a membership number cannot, because that method is off. Match how they log in against what is enabled.
6. On the same tab, read the **ONE-TIME PASSWORD (OTP)** and **PASSWORD POLICY** blocks. **Expected:** **REQUIRE OTP ON LOGIN Yes**, **OTP DELIVERY SMS ▾**, **OTP EXPIRY 10 min ▾**, **OTP LENGTH 6 digits ▾**; then **MINIMUM LENGTH 8 ▾**, **REQUIRE UPPERCASE No**, **REQUIRE SPECIAL CHARACTER No**. OTP is required and sent by SMS, so a member with a dead or wrong mobile number never receives the code, and a code entered after the 10-minute **OTP EXPIRY** has lapsed. The password rules explain a rejected password. The **Session & Limits** and **Allowed Domains** tabs hold the session and domain rules; their contents were not captured in the July 9 corpus, so those are TBD — parked. Do not click **Save Changes**. **[READ-ONLY]**
7. Return to the portal hub and read **Recent Portal Activity**. **Expected:** the feed, subtitled "Last 24 hours", lists member events with a timestamp and channel, such as "Sarah Johnson updated billing info · Today · 2:41 PM · Mobile", "Mike Chen upgraded to Annual Plan · Today · 1:18 PM · Desktop", "3 failed login attempts — tbrown@email.com · Today · 11:04 AM · Mobile", "Lisa Park linked a family account · Today · 9:22 AM · Mobile", and "Portal settings published · Yesterday · 4:55 PM · Admin". The failed-login line is the one you want: it names the account, the count, the time, and whether the attempt came from **Mobile** or **Desktop**. That confirms the member is reaching the login and being rejected, and tells you which channel to test.
8. For a mobile-app complaint, switch modules to **Digital → Mobile App Users** and read the list header. **Expected:** the header reads **Mobile App Users**; the KPI strip shows **4,820 Total Users**, **3,240 Active Monthly**, **58% iOS**, **42% Android**. The **Mobile User List** card ("4 records") carries a **Search** box with placeholder "Search mobile app users…", a **Filters ▾** control, and columns **NAME · EMAIL · APP VERSION · PLATFORM · LAST ACTIVE · NOTIFICATIONS · STATUS**.
9. Find the member's row and read the three columns that explain most app problems. **Expected:** rows such as **Tom Brown · tbrown@email.com · 4.0.5 · Android · 2025-05-25 · On · Active** sit alongside newer ones like **Sarah Johnson · sarah.j@email.com · 4.2.1 · iOS · 2025-06-01 · On · Active**. **APP VERSION** flags an out-of-date build (**4.0.5** against **4.2.1** for others), a common "the app won't let me in" or "a feature is missing" cause. **NOTIFICATIONS Off** (as on Lisa Park's row) explains "I never get alerts". **STATUS** confirms the account is **Active**. Deeper feature flags live on **Mobile App Features** and **Mobile App Branding** in the same **Digital** sidebar; a **Mobile App Preview** to see the member's app view is referenced in the plan but is not in the July 9 corpus, so its location is TBD — parked. Do not click **Export** or **+ New Record**. **[READ-ONLY]**
10. Package the escalation from what you read, and stop. **Expected:** a ticket that states whether the portal is **● Live** and on the right URL, which **LOGIN METHOD** the member used against what is enabled, whether **REQUIRE OTP ON LOGIN** is on and how OTP is delivered, the **Recent Portal Activity** failed-login line (account, count, time, channel), and, for a mobile ticket, the member's **APP VERSION**, **NOTIFICATIONS**, and **STATUS**. For a "can't see a feature" ticket, name the **Features & Modules** "7 of 9" state or the mobile feature page you checked. You change nothing: enabling a login method, flipping a module, or publishing the portal are production actions with the right role, never done from the demo tenant. **[READ-ONLY]**
11. Recap the path. **Expected:** you confirmed the portal was **● Live** and on the right URL and reproduced with **Preview Portal**, checked **Access & Security** for the login method, OTP, and password rules, read **Recent Portal Activity** for the member's failed logins, and, for a mobile ticket, read the member's row on **Mobile App Users**. Every screen was read-only.

## Expected results

You can tell a portal-wide outage from one blocked account by reading the **● Live** chip and the **247 Logins Today** KPI before you navigate. You know where the login rules live (**Settings → Customer Portal → Access & Security → Authentication**: login methods, OTP, password policy), where a member's failed logins are timestamped (**Recent Portal Activity** on the portal hub), where a switched-off portal feature shows up (**Features & Modules**, "7 of 9 enabled"), and where a mobile member's app version, notifications, and status sit (**Digital → Mobile App Users**). You escalate with the portal status and URL, the relevant login setting, the failed-login line, and the mobile row, and you never publish, save, or edit anything.

**Knowledge check:** (1) Which one chip on the portal hub tells you whether any member can reach the portal at all? (2) A member says their membership number is rejected at sign-in. Which setting confirms why, and where does it live? (3) A member "never gets the code" needed to log in. Which two OTP fields explain that? (4) For a "can't see a feature" complaint, what is the fastest thing to check on the portal hub, and what is the mobile-app equivalent?

## Notes and troubleshooting (observed behavior only)

- **● Live** is the portal-wide gate. If the portal is not Live, no member reaches the sign-in and the ticket is not one account. **247 Logins Today** on a Live portal points you at a single member; zero logins on a Live portal points back at the portal itself.
- OTP is the quiet cause of "my password is right but I still can't get in". **REQUIRE OTP ON LOGIN** is Yes and **OTP DELIVERY** is SMS, so a wrong or dead mobile number, or a code entered after the 10-minute **OTP EXPIRY**, blocks the login even when the password is correct.
- Two tabs on **Access & Security** were not captured in the July 9 corpus: **Session & Limits** and **Allowed Domains**. The default **Authentication** tab is fully grounded; the session-timeout detail and any domain allow-list on those two tabs are TBD — parked. The hub **Access & Security** card does show **SESSION TIMEOUT 30 min**.
- The full **Features & Modules** inventory (which two of nine modules are off) lives on the Features & Modules subpage, outside this playbook's captured pages. The hub card grounds the **7 of 9** count and shows **WASH HISTORY On** and **FAMILY PLAN On**; identifying the two disabled modules means opening that subpage. TBD — parked for this playbook.
- The mobile feature flags and the **Mobile App Preview** named in the plan (**Mobile App Features** / **Mobile App Branding** / **Mobile App Preview**) sit on **Settings → Mobile App** pages not captured here. Their contents are TBD — parked; name them as the next stop for a mobile feature-visibility ticket.
- Every page here also shows the session idle behavior ("Still there?" with a 2:00 countdown and a **Stay Active** toast) and the **Ask Shyne** header button. Both are out of scope for this playbook; session behavior is parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #9).
- Read-only throughout: **Publish Changes**, **Save Changes**, **Export**, and **+ New Record** are never clicked. **Preview Portal** is opened read-only to reproduce the member view.
