# WC-PB-08 · Campaign / automation stopped sending (Troubleshooting Playbook)

v1.0 · July 9, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 9, 2026.**

## Purpose

A site manager says a campaign went out to nobody, or a member never got the text the automation was supposed to send, or the marketing lead asks why this month's numbers are flat. This playbook is the order you work that ticket. You read the campaign's own status first, then the deliverability guardrails, then the scheduler that actually fires the sends, then the message log, and finally the billing side, because any one of those can quietly hold a send. You verify at each stop and change nothing. Turning a campaign back on, clearing a suppression rule, or lifting a spending cap are production actions with the right role, never done from the demo tenant.

Work it as a narrowing flow. The **Status** column on the campaign list tells you whether the campaign is even eligible to send. **Deliverability** tells you whether the platform is throttling sends to protect the sender reputation. The **Task Scheduler** tells you whether the send job is running at all. **Mass SMS Log** is the per-message trail. And **Billing → Usage & Billing** is where a spending cap or an open invoice would show up. Every screen here is read-only.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians on Tech Support and Onboarding who take marketing and automation tickets. You should be signed in and comfortable with the global header and the left navigation rail (WC-01-01 and WC-01-02). No marketing-automation background is assumed. This is a diagnosis-and-escalation playbook: you read the surfaces and route the ticket, you do not launch, resume, or reconfigure a campaign.

## Navigation path

Four modules carry this playbook, all reachable from the left rail. **Marketing → Campaigns** for the status chips, **Marketing → Deliverability** for the sender-reputation guardrails, **Settings → Automation → Task Scheduler** and **Settings → Automation → Mass SMS Log** for the send engine and its trail, and **Billing → Usage & Billing** for the spending and invoice angle.

## Steps

One action per step. Hold each new screen two to three seconds before moving on. Nothing in this lesson is created, edited, sent, resumed, or toggled.

1. Open **Marketing → Campaigns** and read the KPI strip across the top. **Expected:** four tiles read **2 Active Campaigns**, **0 Sent This Month**, **— Avg Open Rate**, and **$0 Revenue Attributed**. Read **0 Sent This Month** first: when a manager says "nothing went out," this is the network-level confirmation that sends have stalled, not just one recipient who missed a text. It tells you to keep going rather than chase a single address.
2. Drop to the **Campaign List** card and read the columns and the **Status** values. Do not click a row. **Expected:** the card titled **Campaign List** shows **5 records** with columns **CAMPAIGN NAME · TYPE · STATUS · AUDIENCE · SENT · OPEN RATE · REVENUE**. The status values in view are **Draft**, **Scheduled**, **Active**, and **Completed**. A campaign in **Draft** was never launched and a **Completed** campaign has already finished its run, so neither will send now. Only **Scheduled** and **Active** campaigns are eligible, so those are the rows worth investigating. The same **Status** read applies to automations under **Marketing → Automation**; that sibling list was not captured in the July 9 corpus, so treat its columns as TBD — parked when you get there. **[READ-ONLY]**
3. Look at the **Status** cell on the eligible rows for a weather-pause pill. **Expected:** two rows carry it: **Holiday Referral Blast** (**Scheduled**) and **Summer Unlimited Push** (**Active**) both show **⛅ Weather paused — 6 locations** next to the status dot. This is a built-in hold, not an error: the campaign is eligible but the platform is holding its sends at those six locations. When you see this pill, the "why did it stop" answer is weather-pause, and the ticket should name it. Where weather-pause is configured and exactly what it gates is not observable in the platform and is TBD — parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #6); report the pill and the location count, and route the config question rather than guessing.
4. Go to **Marketing → Deliverability** and read the alert banner at the top. **Expected:** a banner reads **Spam complaint rate elevated — "Flash Sale Weekend" campaign reached 0.18% complaints (threshold: 0.10%). Pausing additional sends recommended.** with a **Review campaign →** link. This is the second reason a campaign can go quiet: the platform's deliverability guardrails recommend pausing sends when complaints or bounces climb, to protect the sending domain. A campaign that looks **Active** can still be held back here.
5. Read the deliverability KPI row and the **List Health Metrics** panel, and leave every toggle alone. **Expected:** the KPI row reads **94.2% Inbox Rate**, **0.42% Hard Bounce Rate**, **0.18% Spam Complaint Rate**, **78 / 100 List Health Score**, and **96.3% TCPA Consent**. Below, **List Health Metrics** lists **Hard Bounce Rate ● GOOD**, **Soft Bounce Rate ● WATCH**, **Spam Complaint Rate ● ACTION**, **Unsubscribe Rate ● GOOD**, **List Engagement (30d) ● GOOD**, and **Never-Engaged Contacts ● WATCH**. The **● ACTION** flag on **Spam Complaint Rate** is what the banner is reacting to. The **Engagement-Based Suppression** section below has live on/off toggles that decide who the platform stops emailing; note whether they are on, but do not flip any of them here. The tabs **List Health · Bounce Management · Suppression List · Domain Warm-Up · TCPA Consent · Settings** are where the detail lives; reading them is safe, changing them is not. **[READ-ONLY]**
6. Go to **Settings → Automation → Task Scheduler** and read the KPI band. **Expected:** four tiles read **18 Total Tasks**, **16 Active**, **2 Running Now**, and **0 Failed**. Read **0 Failed** first: it tells you the scheduling engine itself is healthy, so a stopped campaign is more likely a status or a guardrail hold than a broken job. If **0 Failed** were instead a positive number, the send job crashing would jump to the top of your list.
7. In the **Task Scheduler List** find the **SMS Campaigns** job row and read its cells. **Expected:** the columns are **TASK NAME · SCHEDULE · LAST RUN · NEXT RUN · STATUS**, and the **SMS Campaigns** row reads **Hourly** · **1 hour ago** · **In 52 min** · **Active**. That row is the heartbeat of outbound texting: it ran an hour ago, it runs again in under an hour, and its status is **Active**. If a campaign is eligible and unpaused but still silent, a **SMS Campaigns** job that is not **Active** or whose **LAST RUN** is stale is your evidence. (Note: the list header reads **18 records** and the KPI reads **18 Total Tasks**, but only three rows loaded in the July 9 capture — **Daily Report Email**, **SMS Campaigns**, and **Database Backup** — with the footer showing "Showing 1–3 of 3 records"; the remaining task rows are TBD — parked.) **[READ-ONLY]**
8. Go to **Settings → Automation → Mass SMS Log** and read the columns and the load state. **Expected:** the page is titled **Mass SMS Log List** with a **— Total Entries** KPI, a search box placeholder "Search SMS log entries…", and columns **DATE · PHONE · EMAIL · BARCODE · STATUS**, with **ROWS PER PAGE** offering 25 / 50 / 100. This is the per-message send trail: one row per recipient, with the **STATUS** of each. In the July 9 capture the body returned "Couldn't load SMS log entries (Failed to fetch). Check your connection to the AI agent service." and the counter read 0–0 of 0, so the columns are grounded but the actual send rows are TBD — parked. Treat an empty log the same way on the live tenant: confirm the fetch returned data before telling anyone no messages were sent. Do not click **+ New Record**. **[READ-ONLY]**
9. Go to **Billing → Usage & Billing** and check the spending angle. **Expected:** the page opens on the **Overview** tab with a period line **Current Billing Period — May 2026** and tabs **Overview · SMS Usage · Email Usage · Invoices 1 open · Rate Card · Spending Alerts**. Two of those matter for a stopped send: **Invoices 1 open** shows an unpaid invoice, and **Spending Alerts** is where a usage cap would live. The plan flags a spending cap or an open invoice as a *plausible* gate on sends, so this is a place to check, not a confirmed cause. The **Spending Alerts** tab contents were not captured in the July 9 corpus (only the default **Overview** tab was), so what that tab shows and whether a cap actually blocks sends is TBD — parked; name the tab and the open-invoice count in the ticket rather than asserting the mechanism. Do not click **Export CSV**.
10. Still on **Overview**, read the **Usage by Source** panel to tie spend back to a specific campaign. **Expected:** the panel titled **Usage by Source — May 2026**, subtitle "Cost breakdown by campaign & automation", has columns **SOURCE · TYPE · VOLUME · COST · SHARE** and tags each row **Campaign**, **Automation**, or **Transactional** (for example, **Campaign** **Summer Membership Drive**, **Automation** **Nth Wash Rewards**). If the ticket names a campaign, this panel confirms whether it sent anything this period and at what volume, which either supports or contradicts the "it stopped sending" claim. **[READ-ONLY]**
11. Package the escalation from what you read, and stop. **Expected:** a ticket that names the campaign or automation, its **TYPE** and current **Status** chip (including a **⛅ Weather paused — 6 locations** pill if present), the deliverability signal if the banner is up, and the **SMS Campaigns** scheduler timestamps (**LAST RUN** and **NEXT RUN**). You state which gate you found: eligibility (**Draft** / **Completed**), weather-pause, a deliverability hold, a scheduler problem, or a spending / invoice question. Resuming the campaign, clearing suppression, or lifting a cap happens in production with the right role, never here. **[READ-ONLY]**
12. Recap the path. **Expected:** you read the **Status** chip to see if the campaign was even eligible, checked **Deliverability** for a reputation hold, confirmed the **SMS Campaigns** job was **Active** and on schedule, looked at the **Mass SMS Log** trail, and checked **Billing → Usage & Billing** for a spending or invoice gate. Then you escalated with the campaign name, its status chip, and the scheduler timestamps. Every screen was read-only.

## Expected results

You can tell an eligibility problem from a live hold by reading one column: a **Draft** or **Completed** campaign was never going to send, while a **Scheduled** or **Active** campaign carrying **⛅ Weather paused — 6 locations** is being held on purpose. You know that **Deliverability** can pause sends to protect the sender reputation, that the **SMS Campaigns** row in **Task Scheduler** is the send job to confirm (**0 Failed**, **Active**, **LAST RUN** recent), that **Mass SMS Log** is the per-message trail, and that **Billing → Usage & Billing** is where a spending cap or an open invoice would surface. You escalate with the campaign name, its status chip, the deliverability signal, and the scheduler timestamps, and you move no data.

**Knowledge check:** (1) Which column tells you whether a campaign is even eligible to send, and which two values mean it will not? (2) What does the **⛅ Weather paused — 6 locations** pill mean, and what part of it can you not confirm from the platform? (3) Which single scheduler row proves the outbound-text job is running, and which three cells do you read on it? (4) What goes into the escalation, and which actions do you never take from the demo tenant?

## Notes and troubleshooting (observed behavior only)

- The order matters. **Status** is cheapest to check and rules out the most common cause (a campaign left in **Draft** or already **Completed**), so read it before you open Deliverability or the scheduler. A campaign that never left **Draft** is a training gap, not a platform fault.
- The **⛅ Weather paused — 6 locations** pill is real and appears on both a **Scheduled** row (**Holiday Referral Blast**) and an **Active** row (**Summer Unlimited Push**). The pill and its location count are observable; where weather-pause is configured and precisely what it holds are not, and that stays parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #6). Report the pill; route the config question.
- Deliverability guardrails can stop sends independent of campaign status. The July 9 banner recommends pausing sends because the spam-complaint rate hit **0.18%** against a **0.10%** threshold. That is a legitimate, intended reason a healthy-looking campaign goes quiet. The **Engagement-Based Suppression** toggles on that page decide who the platform stops emailing; treat them as read-only, and never flip one while diagnosing.
- The **SMS Campaigns** scheduler job is the outbound-text heartbeat. In the July 9 capture it was **Active**, ran **1 hour ago**, and was due again **In 52 min**, with the scheduler KPI showing **0 Failed**. A stopped campaign with a healthy scheduler points you back at status or a guardrail, not the engine.
- Two demo-data artifacts to expect, not to file bugs on: the **Task Scheduler** list header claims **18 records** while only three rows load (footer "Showing 1–3 of 3 records"), and the **Campaign Performance** panel breakdown (**2 Active · 1 Draft · 1 Completed**) sums to four while the list holds five records. These are demo-tenant data gaps; the columns, KPIs, and the rows that do load are grounded, and the missing rows are TBD — parked.
- The **Mass SMS Log** body returned "Couldn't load SMS log entries (Failed to fetch). Check your connection to the AI agent service." in the July 9 capture, so the send-trail rows are TBD — parked. On the live tenant, an empty log after a real send may be the same fetch failure rather than proof that nothing sent; confirm the data loaded before you conclude.
- The **Spending Alerts** tab on **Billing → Usage & Billing** was not captured (the corpus holds only the default **Overview** tab), so its contents and whether a spending cap actually blocks sends are TBD — parked. The tab label and the **Invoices 1 open** count are grounded; document those and route the "does a cap gate sends" question rather than asserting it.
- Every page here also shows the **Ask Shyne** header button and the session idle prompt ("Still there?" with a 2:00 countdown). Both are out of scope for this playbook; session behavior is parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #9).
