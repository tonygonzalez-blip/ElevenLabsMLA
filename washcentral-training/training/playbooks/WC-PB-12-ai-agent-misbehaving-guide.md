# WC-PB-12 · AI agent misbehaving, customer-facing (Troubleshooting Playbook)

v1.0 · July 9, 2026 · Author: Claude — Micrologic training project
**Verified against demo.washcentral.com on July 9, 2026.**

## Purpose

A customer says the AI agent gave a wrong answer, argued with them, never handed off to a person, or went quiet when they texted at night. This playbook is the order you work that ticket. You read the conversation, check whether it should have escalated and where a human would pick it up, look for the stale or missing knowledge article behind a bad answer, and confirm the channel itself is healthy. Then you escalate with enough context for the team that owns the agent to fix it.

The agent in question is the customer-facing one under **Settings → AI Agent**: the web, SMS, and voice assistant your customers talk to. It is not **Ask Shyne**, the staff assistant in the header (that one is out of scope here; see PARKED-ITEMS.md #8). Every screen in this playbook is read-only. You never send a test message, retry a channel, edit a knowledge article, or claim an escalation. You read, and you route.

## Audience and prerequisites

Internal Micrologic technicians on Tech Support who take customer-experience tickets about the AI agent. You should be signed in and comfortable with the left navigation rail and the global header (WC-01-01 and WC-01-02). No conversational-AI background is assumed. This is a diagnosis-and-escalation playbook, so you read transcripts, queues, and health panels; you do not configure the agent, send test traffic, or take an escalation yourself.

## Navigation path

Two areas carry this playbook. Four surfaces sit in Settings under the **AI Agent** group: `Settings → AI Agent → Conversations`, `→ Escalations`, `→ Knowledge Base`, and `→ Channels & Deployment`. The fifth is where a person actually takes the handoff: `CRM → Agent Console`, in the **SERVICE DESK** section of the left rail. One more Settings page, `Settings → AI Agent → Analytics`, gives you the containment context you attach to the escalation.

## Steps

One action per step. Hold each new screen two to three seconds before moving on. Nothing in this playbook is created, edited, sent, retried, or claimed.

1. Open `Settings → AI Agent → Conversations`. **Expected:** the breadcrumb reads **Settings › AI Agent · Conversations**, and the **AI Agent** group in the settings nav lists **Overview**, **Configuration**, **Knowledge Base**, **Channels & Deployment**, **Conversations**, **Escalations**, and **Analytics**. This is the log for the customer-facing agent. Start here because the transcript is the ticket: everything else you check confirms or explains what you read in the conversation.
2. Read the Conversations log layout. This is where you pull the transcript. **Expected:** a KPI band across the top reads **Total (30d)**, **Active Now**, **Escalated**, **Avg Handle**, **Est. Cost (30d)**, and **CSAT**; the table columns are **CUSTOMER · CHANNEL · TOPIC · STATUS · MSGS · SENTIMENT · LAST ACTIVITY**; a search box shows the placeholder "Search conversations…" beside **Filters 0** and **Search**. Do not click **Configure Agent** (it opens the agent's configuration) or **Export** (a download). In the July 9 demo tenant this log reads **No conversations found.** with zero KPIs, a demo-data artifact; the aggregate thirty-day counts live on the **Analytics** page (step 12). The view that opens a single conversation's full transcript needs a row to click and was not in the July 9 corpus, so it is TBD — parked. **[READ-ONLY]**
3. Narrow to the reported conversation and note its identifiers. **Expected:** type into "Search conversations…" or open **Filters 0** to match on **CUSTOMER**, **CHANNEL**, or **TOPIC**; the row's **MSGS** count tells you how long the exchange ran and **SENTIMENT** tells you how it felt to the customer. Opening the row would show the full back-and-forth and the conversation's own ID; that detail view is TBD — parked in this corpus. Carry the customer, the channel, and (from the open transcript at record time) the conversation ID into the escalation. **[READ-ONLY]**
4. Click **Escalations** in the **AI Agent** group. This answers "did it hand off when it should have?" **Expected:** the breadcrumb reads **Settings › AI Agent · Escalations**, the panel is labeled **AI AGENT · HUMAN HANDOFF** with the subtitle "7 open · 3 claimed", and the KPI band reads **7 Open**, **3 Claimed**, **6m 12s Avg Wait**, **1 SLA Breach**, **23 Resolved Today**.
5. Read the escalation queue. The **REASON** column is the diagnostic. **Expected:** the columns are **CUSTOMER · CHANNEL · REASON · PRIORITY · STATUS · WAITING · ASSIGNED**, with **PRIORITY** chips **High** / **Medium** / **Low** and **STATUS** chips **Open** / **Claimed** / **Resolved**. Observed reasons include "Negative sentiment · billing dispute" (Voice, High), "Refund above cap ($120)" (Web, High), "Customer asked for a manager" (SMS, Medium), and "Unresolved after 8 turns" (Web, Medium). Those are the guardrails that should trip a handoff. If your customer's chat is here with a sensible **REASON**, the agent escalated correctly and the problem is downstream; if the chat is absent, the agent kept trying when it should have handed off, and that gap is the misbehavior you report. A row with **ASSIGNED** reading "You" is one the signed-in agent has claimed. Do not click **Claim Next** (it assigns the next case to you) or **Escalation Rules** (it opens the rules editor). **[READ-ONLY]**
6. Confirm where a human takes the handoff. Open `CRM → Agent Console` from the **SERVICE DESK** section of the left rail. **Expected:** the breadcrumb reads **Service Desk › Agent Console**; the **Handoff Queue** header shows "0 waiting · avg 0:00 · 0 high priority"; a presence selector top-right offers **Available** / **Away** / **Busy** / **Offline**. This is the live intake: an escalation from step 5 surfaces here for a teammate to pick up.
7. Read the **Handoff Queue** controls. **Expected:** assignment filters **Mine 0** · **Unassigned 0** · **Team 0** · **All 0**; channel chips **All 0** · **Voice 0** · **SMS 0** · **Web 0**; a sort control offering **Longest wait first** / **Priority first** / **Most recent first**; and a search box "Search name, phone, plan, reason…". The July 9 queue is empty, so the panel reads **No matching handoffs.** and **Select a handoff from the queue to open its console.**, with the right-hand **Customer** pane reading **No conversation selected.** Because the queue was empty in the corpus, the open-handoff console (the conversation and customer panes that fill in once a handoff is selected) is TBD — parked. Do not click **Enable desktop notifications** (it triggers a browser permission prompt). **[READ-ONLY]**
8. Go to `Settings → AI Agent → Knowledge Base`. A wrong answer usually traces to a stale or missing article. **Expected:** the breadcrumb reads **Settings › AI Agent · Knowledge Base** under the subtitle "Documents the agent searches to answer customer questions.", with chips **pgvector · text-embedding-3-small**, **Tenant: ewc-demo**, and **● Synced**, and a KPI band reading **Articles**, **Indexed**, **Processing**, **Vector Chunks**, **Categories**, and **Last Sync**.
9. Read the article table and the add controls. **Expected:** the table columns are **TITLE · CATEGORY · STATUS · CHUNKS · UPDATED · ACTIONS**; **STATUS** (Indexed versus Processing) and **UPDATED** tell you whether the article the agent should have used is current and searchable, and "Search articles…" finds the topic the customer asked about. The **ADD KNOWLEDGE** panel offers **Upload Documents** ("Drop files or click to browse", "PDF, DOCX, TXT, MD, CSV · chunked & embedded automatically") and **Add from URL** ("The crawler imports the page text, splits it into chunks, and embeds it for retrieval.", with an **Import URL** button). Do not click **Re-index**, **+ Add Article**, or **Import URL**; each one changes the index. In the July 9 tenant the table reads **No articles yet. Click '+ Add Article' or import a URL above.** with zero counts, and **RETRIEVAL HEALTH** reads "Analytics available after first customer conversations." (the **Analytics** page reports a populated article count for the same tenant, the same demo-data artifact as step 2). **[READ-ONLY]**
10. Go to `Settings → AI Agent → Channels & Deployment`. Decide whether the channel itself is healthy or whether Voice is simply in beta. **Expected:** the breadcrumb reads **Settings › AI Agent · Channels & Deployment** under the subtitle "Provisioning, install, and channel health.", with chips **3 / 3 Live**, **99.95% uptime**, and **Tenant: atlas-fl**, and a KPI strip reading **Web Chat Live**, **SMS Live**, **Voice Beta**, **99.95% 30d Uptime**, and **412 ms Avg Latency**.
11. Read the three channel health cards. **Expected:** **WEB CHAT** carries an **INSTALLED & LIVE** tag with **Embed Snippet** (**Copy**), **Allowed Domains**, **Detected Installs**, health chips **Widget loads** / **WebSocket connected** / **Key valid**, and a **↗ Preview Live Widget** button. **SMS** is **LIVE** with **Provisioned Number**, **A2P 10DLC Registration**, **Throughput**, and **Opt-out Compliance** ("STOP / HELP auto-handled"). **VOICE** is **BETA** with **Provisioned Number**, **Transport**, **STT / TTS**, **Max Concurrent Calls**, and the note "Voice is in beta. Recording consent disclosure plays on every call." Two reads matter for this ticket: Voice being **Beta** sets the expectation for rougher voice behavior, and SMS delivery windows explain "it ignored my text at night" (the **AI Agent → Overview** page flags **TCPA quiet hours on** for the SMS channel). Do NOT click **Send Test Message**, **Send Test SMS**, or **Place Test Call**: those send real traffic to real numbers, and this playbook never sends test prompts (PARKED-ITEMS.md #8). Leave **Run Health Check** and **Channel Settings** alone as well. **[READ-ONLY]**
12. Go to `Settings → AI Agent → Analytics` for the containment context. **Expected:** the breadcrumb reads **Settings › AI Agent · Analytics** under the title **Agent Analytics** and subtitle "Performance, deflection, and usage.", scoped by the chips **Last 30 days**, **Atlas Car Wash**, and **Tenant: atlas-fl**. The KPI band reads **3,842 Conversations**, **78% Containment**, **3.7% Escalation Rate**, **4.6 CSAT**, **2m 14s Avg Handle**, and **$214 Est. Cost**, and a **Containment by channel** panel breaks out **Web 81%**, **SMS 76%**, and **Voice 68%**. **Containment Rate** is the share of AI-agent conversations resolved without a human handoff; a channel whose containment has slipped is real evidence the agent is struggling there. Do not click **Export**. **[READ-ONLY]**
13. Package the escalation and stop. **Expected:** a ticket that names the conversation ID, the channel it happened on, and the containment context you read on the **Analytics** page, plus which check pointed to the cause: a missing **Escalations** row (it should have handed off), a stale or missing **Knowledge Base** article (bad answer), or a channel state such as **Voice Beta** or an SMS quiet-hours window (silence or roughness). You changed nothing on any screen. Diagnosis and routing are your job on this playbook; configuration, knowledge edits, and channel changes happen with the right role, upstream. **[READ-ONLY]**

## Expected results

You can take a "the AI agent misbehaved" ticket and work it in order: pull the transcript in **Conversations**, check **Escalations** to see whether it handed off and read the **REASON**, confirm the handoff lands in the **CRM → Agent Console** queue, look for the stale or missing article in the **Knowledge Base**, and read channel health in **Channels & Deployment** (Voice is Beta; SMS has quiet hours). You escalate with the conversation ID, the channel, and the containment context from **Analytics**, and you never send a test message, edit an article, or claim a case.

**Knowledge check:** (1) Which page tells you whether the agent handed a conversation off when it should have, and which column is the reason? (2) Where does an escalated chat land for a human to pick up? (3) What is the most common cause of a wrong answer, and where do you check it? (4) What is Containment Rate, and where do you read it? (5) Name three controls on these screens you must never click, and why.

## Notes and troubleshooting (observed behavior only)

- The customer-facing agent under **Settings → AI Agent** and the staff **Ask Shyne** assistant in the header are two different tools. This playbook is only about the customer-facing one. Ask Shyne is send-type and out of scope (PARKED-ITEMS.md #8).
- Two surfaces in this playbook read empty in the July 9 demo tenant: the **Conversations** log ("No conversations found.") and the **Knowledge Base** table ("No articles yet."), both with zero KPIs, while the **Overview** and **Analytics** pages show populated thirty-day counts (3,842 conversations, a populated article count, 78% containment). That KPI-versus-table gap is a known demo-data artifact, not a platform fault; confirm the log actually returned data before telling a customer there is no record of their chat.
- The single-conversation transcript view (opening a **Conversations** row) and the open-handoff console (the **Agent Console** panes after a handoff is selected) were not captured, because the demo log and queue were empty. Record those against live data at record time; where a value cannot be shown, hold the parked card. Both are TBD — parked.
- **REASON** on the **Escalations** page is the fastest read for "should it have escalated?" The four observed reasons ("Negative sentiment · billing dispute", "Refund above cap ($120)", "Customer asked for a manager", "Unresolved after 8 turns") are exactly the situations a well-behaved agent hands off. A chat that is missing from this queue when it clearly hit one of those situations is the misbehavior to report.
- Channel state changes the diagnosis. **Voice** is labeled **Beta**, so rougher voice behavior is expected and worth setting the customer's expectation on. The SMS card's **Opt-out Compliance** reads "STOP / HELP auto-handled", and the **AI Agent → Overview** page flags **TCPA quiet hours on** for SMS: a customer texting inside the quiet-hours window can look like the agent ignored them.
- The **Knowledge Base** shows a tenant chip of **ewc-demo**, while **Channels & Deployment** and **Analytics** show **atlas-fl** (and **Atlas Car Wash**). The tenant labels are inconsistent across the AI Agent pages in the demo; treat the tenant on the page you are reading as the source for that page, and flag the mismatch rather than assuming one is authoritative.
- Every page here also shows the session idle behavior ("Still there?" with a 2:00 countdown and a "Stay Active" toast); that is out of scope for this playbook and is parked (PARKED-ITEMS.md #9). If it appears mid-record, dismiss it off-camera and re-roll the segment.
